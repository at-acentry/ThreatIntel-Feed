Microsoft's May 2026 Patch Tuesday landed last night with 120 fixes and no zero-days, but four merit immediate attention: a wormable Netlogon RCE (CVE-2026-41089, CVSS 9.8), an unauthenticated DNS Client heap overflow (CVE-2026-41096, CVSS 9.8), a critical SharePoint deserialization RCE (CVE-2026-40365, CVSS 9.8), and a Windows GDI EMF flaw triggerable via Paint. Ivanti EPMM customers must verify deployment of the May 7 patch for CVE-2026-6973 — CISA's KEV deadline expired May 10 and limited active exploitation is confirmed. On the actor front, Salt Typhoon disclosures continue (Norway, Singapore confirmed Q1; Senator Cantwell pressing AT&T/Verizon on undisclosed Mandiant assessments), and DPRK Lazarus/Kimsuky joint operations remain the dominant cryptocurrency theft and zero-day exploitation threat after $577M in Q1 thefts.

## Executive Summary

- **CVE-2026-0300 (PAN-OS User-ID Auth Portal, CVSS 9.3) — Patch window opens TODAY.** First fixed PAN-OS builds ship May 13 with phased rollout through May 28. Acentry's recommended sequence: validate mitigation hardening, then move PA-Series → VM-Series, internet-facing first. State-sponsored exploitation confirmed by Palo Alto PSIRT.
- **CVE-2026-41089 (Windows Netlogon, CVSS 9.8) — Wormable RCE on domain controllers.** Stack-based buffer overflow allows unauthenticated network code execution against Windows DC role; no zero-day exploitation observed, but the attack profile (network-reachable, no auth, DC-targeting) makes this the priority Patch Tuesday item.
- **CVE-2026-41096 (Windows DNS Client, CVSS 9.8) — Heap overflow on every Windows endpoint.** Malicious DNS response from attacker-controlled or hijacked resolver yields unauthenticated RCE on the client; weaponization risk on coffee-shop / hotel Wi-Fi and compromised internal recursive resolvers is high.
- **CVE-2026-40365 (SharePoint Server 2019, CVSS 9.8) — Unauthenticated deserialization RCE.** Pre-auth network-reachable RCE on on-prem SharePoint farms; pattern matches prior SharePoint exploitation waves where weaponization landed within 7–14 days. Patch KB5002870 now.
- **CVE-2026-6973 (Ivanti EPMM, CVSS 7.2) — KEV deadline passed; limited exploitation confirmed.** Authenticated admin RCE chained with credentials harvested via prior CVE-2026-1340 exploitation. On-prem EPMM only; Ivanti Neurons (cloud) unaffected. Patches available in 12.6.1.1 / 12.7.0.1 / 12.8.0.1 (May 7).
- **CVE-2026-31431 "Copy Fail" (Linux kernel, CVSS 7.8) — KEV deadline Friday May 15.** All major distros patched; container hosts and multi-tenant cloud workloads remain the highest-priority remediation surface. Public PoC is reliable and race-free.
- **DPRK Lazarus + Kimsuky joint operations — $577M Q1 2026 cryptocurrency theft.** Drift Protocol ($285M) and KelpDAO ($293M) thefts trace to UNC4736 / Citrine Sleet / AppleJeus social-engineering pipeline. Lazarus continues to account for ~76% of all 2026 hack losses to date.

## APT / Threat Actor Activity

### Salt Typhoon (aliases: GhostEmperor, FamousSparrow variants, RedMike — Chinese MSS)

- **Targets:** Telecommunications providers, ISPs, internet exchange points, maritime/subsea infrastructure, and (as of Q1 2026) named European telecom operators. Norway (PST disclosure, Feb 2026) and Singapore have publicly attributed compromises. Total scope continues to expand from the FBI's August 2025 baseline of 200 organizations / 80 countries.
- **TTPs:** Initial access via unpatched edge appliances — Cisco IOS XE, Palo Alto NGFW, Fortinet FortiGate (`T1190`); living-off-the-land via PowerShell (`T1059.001`), WMIC (`T1047`), and registry tunneling (`T1112`); custom Go-based SFTP client for staged exfiltration (`T1048.002`); persistent modification of provider-edge routers (`T1542`); lawful-intercept infrastructure abuse for metadata harvest (`T1119`).
- **Recent activity:** Norwegian PST disclosure (Feb 2026) was the first European public attribution. Singapore confirmed parallel compromises in the same window. Senator Cantwell publicly pressed AT&T and Verizon CEOs in February 2026 to produce Mandiant network-assessment reports that the senators allege were withheld — sustained Congressional scrutiny signals the campaign remains an active, unresolved access problem inside US telecoms. CISA AA25-239a indicators remain in-scope for hunt across telecom and maritime defense networks.
- **Attribution confidence:** **High** — joint advisory by CISA, FBI, NSA, and Five Eyes partners; PRC MSS attribution corroborated by Norway PST, Mandiant, and Silent Push infrastructure mapping.
- **Sources:** [CISA AA25-239a](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa25-239a) · [Dark Reading — CISA Telecom Guidance](https://www.darkreading.com/cyberattacks-data-breaches/cisa-issue-guidance-telecoms-salt-typhoon-threat) · [Wikipedia — Salt Typhoon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salt_Typhoon)

### Lazarus + Kimsuky (DPRK Reconnaissance General Bureau joint ops; Lazarus aliases: Hidden Cobra, APT38, Diamond Sleet, AppleJeus, Citrine Sleet / UNC4736)

- **Targets:** Cryptocurrency exchanges, DeFi protocols, custodial wallets, and crypto-adjacent fintech (Lazarus financial track); defense, aerospace, nuclear, and policy/think-tank targets (Kimsuky espionage track). Increasing operational fusion between the two RGB units through shared servers, intelligence, and exploit tooling.
- **TTPs:** Long-cycle social engineering against engineering/finance staff (`T1566.003`) via LinkedIn and recruiter personas — months-long credential and access-development pipelines before the financial event; weaponized job-assessment archives and trojanized developer tooling; **CookiePlus** modular memory-resident backdoor masquerading as the Notepad++ `ComparePlus` plugin (`T1574.001`); shellcode/DLL execution from RSA-encrypted second-stage payloads delivered via **Ranid Downloader** (`T1620`); zero-day chaining as the Kimsuky-Lazarus joint pattern (zero-day surface → exfil → financial event).
- **Recent activity:** **Drift Protocol theft — $285M (April 1, 2026)** attributed with medium confidence to UNC4736 (AppleJeus / Citrine Sleet / Golden Chollima / Gleaming Pisces); the operation was a six-month social-engineering build-up beginning Fall 2025. **KelpDAO theft — $293M** earlier in Q1 takes combined first-quarter DPRK crypto theft to $577M, on a trajectory that exceeds 2024's $6B total. Lazarus + Kimsuky joint ops account for ~76% of all 2026 hack losses to date.
- **Attribution confidence:** **High** for Lazarus crypto track (DOJ indictments, Treasury sanctions, multi-vendor consensus); **Medium-High** for specific UNC4736 sub-cluster attribution on the Drift incident.
- **Sources:** [The Hacker News — Drift $285M](https://thehackernews.com/2026/04/285-million-drift-hack-traced-to-six.html) · [38 North — DPRK Crypto Kleptocracy](https://www.38north.org/2026/01/from-digital-kleptocracy-to-rogue-crypto-superpower/) · [MITRE ATT&CK G0032 (Lazarus)](https://attack.mitre.org/groups/G0032/) · [MITRE ATT&CK G0094 (Kimsuky)](https://attack.mitre.org/groups/G0094/)

### Akira (financially motivated; ransomware-as-a-service operator)

- **Targets:** Mid-market enterprise across manufacturing, professional services, food production, and (per April 2026 CISA update) US critical infrastructure verticals. Recent late-week disclosure sites (May 11, 2026) listed Clarkson Walsh & Coulter, Circle U Foods, and Nijborg Staal.
- **TTPs:** Initial access via SonicWall SSL VPN credential abuse (`T1078`) and exploited edge appliances (`T1190`); Cloudflare Tunnel (`cloudflared.exe`) deployed for command-and-control and persistent remote access (`T1572`); SMB/WMI lateral movement (`T1021.002`, `T1047`); credential dumping via LSASS access (`T1003.001`); large-scale data exfiltration over HTTPS prior to encryption (`T1567.002`); double-extortion with leak-site publication.
- **Recent activity:** CISA's #StopRansomware Akira advisory (AA24-109A) refresh on November 13, 2025 elevated the group to "imminent threat to critical infrastructure"; downloadable STIX IOC packs remain authoritative. Activity tempo through Q1–Q2 2026 has not slowed.
- **Attribution confidence:** **High** (criminal RaaS; not nation-state) — CISA, FBI, NCSC-NL, Europol consensus.
- **Sources:** [CISA AA24-109A (#StopRansomware: Akira)](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa24-109a) · [Ransomware.live — Akira](https://www.ransomware.live/group/akira) · [Darktrace — Akira SonicWall Campaign](https://www.darktrace.com/blog/inside-akiras-sonicwall-campaign-darktraces-detection-and-response)
