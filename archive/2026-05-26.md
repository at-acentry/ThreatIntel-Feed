> **Daily Brief.** The week closes with two compounding pressure points: the actively-exploited NGINX Rift heap overflow (CVE-2026-42945) continues to see in-the-wild exploitation across internet-facing web infrastructure, while the fallout from the May 11 Mini Shai-Hulud supply chain worm—which compromised TanStack, Mistral AI, and 160+ npm/PyPI packages—broadens as GitHub confirms ~3,800 internal repositories were breached downstream. Nation-state activity remains elevated across all major threat actor clusters: Volt Typhoon persists in pre-positioning operations against U.S. critical infrastructure, Salt Typhoon has expanded telecom targeting to South American networks, and an Iranian-affiliated APT continues actively disrupting exposed Rockwell Automation PLCs across U.S. energy, water, and manufacturing sectors. The Remus infostealer (a 64-bit Lumma successor using blockchain-based C2) maintains a large, active infrastructure footprint with new domains confirmed through Ethereum contract analysis.

## Executive Summary

- **CVE-2026-42945 (NGINX Rift)** — An 18-year-old heap buffer overflow in `ngx_http_rewrite_module` (CVSS 9.2) enabling unauthenticated RCE is being actively exploited in the wild; all nginx deployments prior to patched versions should be treated as compromised-until-patched.
- **Mini Shai-Hulud supply chain worm** — TeamPCP's self-propagating CI/CD worm (CVE-2026-45321) compromised 170+ npm packages and 2 PyPI packages on May 11; GitHub confirmed ~3,800 internal repos breached; organizations using `@tanstack/*`, Mistral AI, UiPath, or OpenSearch npm packages should audit CI/CD secrets immediately.
- **Remus Infostealer** campaign remains active with a distributed infrastructure across 15+ ASNs, using Ethereum smart contracts (EtherHiding) to rotate C2 endpoints; new domains and IPs confirmed by independent blockchain analysis.
- **Iranian-affiliated APT** targeting internet-exposed Rockwell Automation/Allen-Bradley PLCs across U.S. critical infrastructure sectors (energy, water, healthcare, manufacturing) since at least March 2026; CISA advisory AA26-097A active.
- **Volt Typhoon** confirmed still active in U.S. critical infrastructure pre-positioning; Salt Typhoon expanded to South American telecom operators with new implant families (TernDoor, PeerTime, BruteEntry).
- **AiTM "code of conduct" phishing campaign** targeted 35,000+ users across 26 countries, predominantly U.S.-based, using session-hijacking proxies to bypass MFA; healthcare and financial services sectors most targeted.

## APT / Threat Actor Activity

### Volt Typhoon (aliases: Bronze Silhouette, Vanguard Panda, DEV-0391, UNC3236, Voltzite, Insidious Taurus)

- **Targets:** U.S. critical infrastructure — communications, energy, transportation, water/wastewater; Guam (strategic U.S. military hub); Asia-Pacific government and private sector
- **TTPs:** Living-off-the-land (LOLBins) exclusively — no custom malware; lateral movement via stolen admin credentials; C2 through compromised SOHO routers and edge devices; initial access via vulnerable Fortinet devices and internet-exposed VPN appliances; use of FOFA for target reconnaissance (`T1595.002`); credential dumping (`T1003`); SOHO router proxy chaining (`T1090.003`); hands-on-keyboard command execution (`T1059.003`)
- **Recent activity:** Confirmed still active in U.S. critical infrastructure as of May 2026 per Dragos and Cybelangel analysis; group is assessed to be in a long-term pre-positioning phase to enable future destructive or disruptive action against OT assets; no new destructive incidents confirmed this reporting period
- **Attribution confidence:** High — attributed to PRC state-sponsored actors by CISA, NSA, FBI, and Five Eyes partners
- **Sources:** [CISA Advisory AA24-038A](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa24-038a), [Cybelangel 2026](https://cybelangel.com/blog/volt-typhoon/), [MITRE ATT&CK G1017](https://attack.mitre.org/groups/G1017/)

### Salt Typhoon (aliases: Earth Estries, GhostEmperor, FamousSparrow, UNC2286)

- **Targets:** Telecommunications providers globally — U.S., Europe, South America (newly confirmed 2026); U.S. congressional communications (Dec 2025)
- **TTPs:** Custom implants — **TernDoor** (persistent backdoor), **PeerTime** (encrypted P2P C2 relay), **BruteEntry** (credential brute-forcing tool); exploitation of known CVEs in edge devices as initial vector (`T1190`); living-off-the-land for lateral movement (`T1021`); lawful intercept system compromise for intelligence collection (`T1557`); abuse of basic configuration errors per FBI Feb 2026 advisory
- **Recent activity:** FBI confirmed February 2026 that Salt Typhoon threats remain active and ongoing; group has hacked at least 200 companies across 80 countries (FBI, August 2025); December 2025 intrusions into multiple U.S. House of Representatives committees confirmed; 2026 expansion into South American telecom networks with new TernDoor/PeerTime/BruteEntry implant suite; Norwegian intelligence agency (PST) disclosed Norway was targeted
- **Attribution confidence:** High — attributed to PRC Ministry of State Security
- **Sources:** [The Record](https://therecord.media/norawy-intelligence-discloses-salt-typhoon-attacks), [Eclypsium](https://eclypsium.com/blog/the-rise-of-chinese-apt-campaigns-volt-typhoon-salt-typhoon-flax-typhoon-and-velvet-ant/), [Vectra AI](https://www.vectra.ai/resources/vectra-ai-threat-briefing-salt-typhoon)

### Iranian-Affiliated APT — ICS/OT Campaign (aliases: Cyber Av3ngers-linked cluster; IRGC-affiliated)

- **Targets:** U.S. critical infrastructure — water/wastewater, energy, healthcare, manufacturing; specifically internet-exposed Rockwell Automation/Allen-Bradley CompactLogix and Micro850 PLCs

- **Sources:** [CISA AA26-097A](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa26-097a), [Dark Reading](https://www.darkreading.com/ics-ot-security/iranian-threat-actors-us-critical-infrastructure-exposed-plcs), [Picus Security](https://www.picussecurity.com/resource/blog/cisa-alert-aa26-097a-iranian-affiliated-actors-target-plcs-across-us-critical-infrastructure)

### TanStack / Mistral AI / UiPath / OpenSearch — Mini Shai-Hulud Worm (TeamPCP)

- **Attack type:** CI/CD pipeline injection via GitHub Actions cache poisoning; self-propagating supply chain worm
- **Ecosystem:** npm (primary), PyPI (secondary); GitHub Actions CI/CD
- **Package(s) affected:** 42 `@tanstack/*` packages (84 malicious versions published in 6-minute window); Mistral AI npm packages; UiPath npm packages; OpenSearch npm packages; 170+ npm packages total; 2 PyPI packages; 404 malicious versions total
- **Impact:** Malicious `router_init.js` payload harvests GitHub tokens, npm tokens, CI/CD secrets, cloud credentials (AWS/GCP/Azure), API keys, SSH keys, cryptocurrency wallets, and AI tool configurations; exfiltration via triple-channel C2 architecture (typosquat domain `git-tanstack.com`, Session messenger network via getsession.org seed nodes, GitHub API dead drops); persistent `gh-token-monitor` daemon installed on developer machines (macOS LaunchAgent or Linux systemd); GitHub disclosed May 19 that ~3,800 internal repositories were breached after stolen tokens were used; campaign achieved a security first by compromising packages with valid SLSA Build Level 3 provenance attestations
- **IOCs:** Malicious domain `git-tanstack.com`; IP `83.142.209.194`; `router_init.js` SHA-256: `ab4fcadaec49c03278063dd269ea5eef82d24f2124a8e15d7b90f2fa8601266c`; GitHub Dune-themed repository dead drops; `gh-token-monitor` systemd/LaunchAgent entries
- **Mitigation:** Immediately audit all CI/CD pipelines for the presence of `gh-token-monitor` daemons; revoke and rotate all GitHub tokens, npm tokens, cloud credentials, and API keys in affected environments; pin all `@tanstack/*` packages to known-good versions prior to May 11, 2026 19:20 UTC; verify lock file integrity; block `git-tanstack.com` and `83.142.209.194` at DNS/proxy layer; review `pull_request_target` GitHub Actions workflow permissions and disable privileged CI runs on fork PRs
- **CVE:** CVE-2026-45321
- **Source:** [Wiz Blog](https://www.wiz.io/blog/mini-shai-hulud-strikes-again-tanstack-more-npm-packages-compromised), [StepSecurity](https://www.stepsecurity.io/blog/mini-shai-hulud-is-back-a-self-spreading-supply-chain-attack-hits-the-npm-ecosystem), [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/05/mini-shai-hulud-worm-compromises.html), [Orca Security](https://orca.security/resources/blog/tanstack-npm-supply-chain-worm/)

## Recommended Actions

- [ ] **SOC:** Hunt for `AF_ALG` socket use on Linux endpoints — on-demand loading (300+ seconds post-boot) is a Copy Fail (CVE-2026-31431) exploitation indicator; review auth.log for malformed `su` records with missing invoking usernames; query for outbound connections to all Remus C2 IPs and domains listed above; detect `gh-token-monitor` processes/services (Mini Shai-Hulud persistence); enable detections for `ngx_http_rewrite_module` crash patterns (CVE-2026-42945)
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt:** Immediately patch NGINX to version addressing CVE-2026-42945 (CVSS 9.2, actively exploited); apply May 2026 Patch Tuesday updates covering 118 CVEs including CVE-2026-41103 (Microsoft SSO Plugin for Jira/Confluence, CVSS 9.1); patch all cPanel/WHM instances for CVE-2026-41940 (CVSS 9.8, exploited since Feb 2026); apply Linux kernel patches for CVE-2026-31431 (Copy Fail — KEV deadline applies to federal agencies); patch Cisco FMC for CVE-2026-20131 (Interlock ransomware exploitation); patch Trend Micro Apex One for CVE-2026-34926 (KEV); patch Langflow for CVE-2025-34291 (KEV); audit any internet-exposed PLCs and remove from public internet per CISA AA26-097A
- [ ] **Dev / AppSec:** Immediately audit all CI/CD pipelines for `gh-token-monitor` daemons introduced by Mini Shai-Hulud; rotate all GitHub tokens, npm tokens, and cloud credentials stored in CI/CD environments; pin `@tanstack/*` packages to pre-May 11 2026 19:20 UTC versions; re-lock and regenerate all package lock files in affected repositories; restrict `pull_request_target` GitHub Actions workflows to prevent fork-based cache poisoning; block `git-tanstack.com` at DNS layer; review Laravel Lang localization package dependencies for credential-stealing payload (Composer packages via abused GitHub version tags)
- [ ] **Leadership:** Elevated risk from Iranian APT targeting OT/ICS — any client with internet-exposed Rockwell Automation PLCs should be contacted for immediate remediation; Mini Shai-Hulud GitHub breach (~3,800 repos) may affect downstream software supply chain beyond confirmed npm packages — advisable to inventory GitHub OAuth app authorizations; AiTM phishing campaign bypass of standard MFA is a material control gap — phishing-resistant MFA (FIDO2/passkeys) deployment should be prioritized for high-value accounts in healthcare and financial services sectors
