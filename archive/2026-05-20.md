> **Daily Brief.** Today's threat landscape is dominated by active exploitation of enterprise network appliances — Cisco SD-WAN and FortiClient EMS are both under targeted attack, with threat actors pivoting quickly from authentication bypass to full control-plane access. Chinese state-sponsored groups Salt Typhoon and Volt Typhoon continue persistent, low-and-slow campaigns against Western telecoms and critical infrastructure, while North Korea's Lazarus Group has pivoted to a new macOS-focused social engineering vector ("Mach-O Man") following its April $290M KelpDAO crypto heist. Ransomware actors Scattered Spider and DragonForce are expanding their retail and financial sector targeting through helpdesk impersonation and MFA fatigue. Security teams should prioritize Cisco SD-WAN and Ivanti EPMM patches and hunt for XenShell JSP webshell artifacts on network appliances.

## Executive Summary

- **CVE-2026-20182 (Cisco SD-WAN, CVSS 10.0):** Actively exploited authentication bypass in Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Controller; threat actor UAT-8616 deploying XenShell JSP webshell post-exploitation — immediate patching required.
- **CVE-2026-1281 / CVE-2026-1340 (Ivanti EPMM, CVSS 9.8):** Twin zero-days enabling unauthenticated RCE; Unit 42 observing widespread automated exploitation attempts globally.
- **CVE-2026-35616 (FortiClient EMS, CVSS 9.1):** Emergency-patched API auth bypass actively exploited since March 31; attackers executing arbitrary commands as SYSTEM on unpatched endpoints.
- **Salt Typhoon:** FBI confirmed in February 2026 the group remains active across 80+ countries, with ongoing telecom intrusions feeding real-time surveillance of targets' communications and movements.
- **Lazarus Group "Mach-O Man":** New macOS campaign targeting fintech/crypto executives via ClickFix fake-meeting lures, following the group's ~$290M KelpDAO heist attributed to April 2026.
- **AI-Assisted Zero-Day Discovery:** Google Threat Intelligence Group confirmed a criminal actor used AI to develop a zero-day 2FA bypass intended for mass exploitation of Google products — proactive counter-discovery disrupted the campaign.

## APT / Threat Actor Activity

### Salt Typhoon (aliases: Earth Estries, FamousSparrow, GhostEmperor, UNC2286)

- **Targets:** Global telecommunications providers, government agencies, technology companies, defense contractors, transportation providers; 80+ countries confirmed as of February 2026.
- **TTPs:** Exploitation of known CVEs in VPN/firewall appliances for initial access (`T1190`); living-off-the-land with PowerShell and WMIC (`T1059.001`); registry manipulation and Windows service creation for persistence (`T1543.003`); credential harvesting from telecom Call Data Records for target tracking (`T1005`).
- **Recent activity:** FBI confirmed February 2026 the group remains active and ongoing. Breached 50+ telecoms across 42 countries in early 2026. Stolen CDR and lawful intercept data provides Chinese intelligence services real-time visibility into targets' communications and physical movements. Chinese tech firms have been linked to supplying infrastructure used in Salt Typhoon campaigns.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — confirmed by FBI, CISA, allied partner agencies; multiple vendor corroborations.
- **Sources:** [CISA Advisory](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa25-239a), [Vectra AI](https://www.vectra.ai/resources/vectra-ai-threat-briefing-salt-typhoon), [Infosecurity Magazine](https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/news/chinese-tech-firms-salt-typhoon/)

### Volt Typhoon (aliases: BRONZE SILHOUETTE, Vanguard Panda, DEV-0391, Voltzite, Insidious Taurus)

- **Targets:** U.S. and allied critical infrastructure — energy, water, transportation, maritime; active since at least 2021.
- **TTPs:** Heavy use of living-off-the-land binaries (LOLBins) to blend with legitimate traffic (`T1218`); exploitation of SOHO router/edge device vulnerabilities for ingress relay networks (`T1190`); Active Directory enumeration (`T1069.002`); long-dwell pre-positioning for potential disruptive action (`T1074`).
- **Recent activity:** Confirmed active in U.S. critical infrastructure in 2026 per CybelAngel. Australia's ASIO Director-General cited Volt Typhoon intrusion attempts against Australian telecommunications networks in November 2025. Focus assessed as pre-positioning for potential disruption in a Taiwan-strait contingency.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — attributed by Five Eyes joint advisory, Microsoft, Mandiant, CrowdStrike.
- **Sources:** [MITRE ATT&CK G1017](https://attack.mitre.org/groups/G1017/), [Picus Security](https://www.picussecurity.com/resource/blog/volt-typhoon-living-off-the-land-cyber-espionage), [CybelAngel](https://cybelangel.com/blog/volt-typhoon/)

### Lazarus Group (aliases: HIDDEN COBRA, Guardians of Peace, APT38, BlueNoroff — DPRK)

- **Targets:** Cryptocurrency exchanges, DeFi protocols, fintech firms, defense-sector individuals; global.
- **TTPs:** "Mach-O Man" / ClickFix campaign — lures targets via fake online meeting invitations, executes malicious macOS binaries (`T1204.002`); social engineering of executives at crypto and fintech firms (`T1566.003`); smart contract exploitation in DeFi protocols; blockchain laundering through mixers and chain-hopping (`T1020`).
- **Recent activity:** April 2026 — attributed by blockchain security firms to the $290M KelpDAO/LayerZero exploit (largest 2026 crypto heist to date). Coindesk documented the new "Mach-O Man" macOS attack vector in April 2026. The group has stolen an estimated $6.75B+ in cryptocurrency since 2017, funding DPRK's weapons program. North Korea's crypto theft is documented to account for 76% of all 2026 DeFi hack losses.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — FBI, OFAC, blockchain analytics firms (Elliptic, Chainalysis); prior pattern-of-life matching.
- **Sources:** [UPI / KelpDAO](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2026/04/22/KelpDAO-LayerZero-North-Korea-crypto-hack-theft-Lazarus-Group/6151776848419/), [Coindesk](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/04/22/lazarus-group-has-become-especially-dangerous-with-new-mach-o-man-attack-certik), [CryptoImpactHub](https://cryptoimpacthub.com/north-korea-6-billion-crypto-theft-dprk-lazarus-2026/)

### Scattered Spider + DragonForce RaaS (aliases: Octo Tempest, UNC3944, 0ktapus)

- **Targets:** Retail, hospitality, telecommunications, financial services; UK and North American focus in recent campaigns.
- **TTPs:** Helpdesk impersonation and phone-based social engineering to reset MFA (`T1078.003`); SIM swapping to bypass 2FA (`T1111`); Okta/SSO credential harvesting via typosquatted vendor domains (`T1566.002`); deployment of DragonForce RaaS for ESXi encryption post-access (`T1486`); living-off-the-land post-exploitation (`T1218`).
- **Recent activity:** Partnership with DragonForce cartel (Conti-linked) confirmed in 2025 Marks & Spencer breach. Group expanded rapidly: 182 victims in Q1 2026 (from 35 in Q4 2025). 81%+ of Scattered Spider phishing domains impersonate tech vendors (Okta, VPN providers, IT helpdesks). ShinyHunters (suspected overlap) was the most active ransomware actor in April 2026 with 15 attributed attacks.
- **Attribution confidence:** Medium-High — CrowdStrike, Darktrace, Acronis analysis; multiple FBI/CISA advisories.
- **Sources:** [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/deep-dive-into-dragonforce-ransomware-and-its-scattered-spider-connection/), [CISA Advisory](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa23-320a), [Acronis](https://www.acronis.com/en/tru/posts/the-dragonforce-cartel-scattered-spider-at-the-gate/)

## YARA Rules

```yara
/*
  Rule 1: XenShell JSP Webshell — CVE-2026-20182 Post-Exploitation Artifact
  Deployed by UAT-8616 on compromised Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN controllers.
  Verified by Cisco Talos: https://blog.talosintelligence.com/sd-wan-ongoing-exploitation/
*/
rule Acentry_XenShell_JSP_Webshell {
    meta:
        author      = "Cisco Talos / Acentry TI"
        date        = "2026-05-20"
        description = "Detects XenShell JSP webshell deployed post-exploitation of CVE-2026-20182 on Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN controllers"
        reference   = "https://blog.talosintelligence.com/sd-wan-ongoing-exploitation/"
        tlp         = "CLEAR"
    strings:
        $shell_name  = "XenShell" ascii nocase
        $jsp_runtime = "Runtime.getRuntime().exec(" ascii
        $jsp_builder = "ProcessBuilder(" ascii
        $jsp_import  = "javax.servlet.http" ascii
        $req_param   = ".getParameter(" ascii
    condition:
        filesize < 512KB and
        $jsp_import and
        (
            $shell_name or
            ($jsp_runtime and $req_param) or
            ($jsp_builder and $req_param)
        )
}


/*
  Rule 2: AsyncRAT Default TLS Certificate Fingerprint
  Commonly observed in community-submitted samples via MalwareBazaar.
  AsyncRAT uses a default self-signed certificate with a predictable CN.
*/
rule Acentry_AsyncRAT_Default_Certificate {
    meta:
        author      = "Abuse.ch Community / Acentry TI"
        date        = "2026-05-20"
        description = "Detects AsyncRAT RAT samples or traffic via default TLS certificate strings and known stub artifact"
        reference   = "https://bazaar.abuse.ch/browse/"
        tlp         = "CLEAR"
    strings:
        $cert_cn   = "AsyncRAT Server" ascii
        $stub_data = "stub_data" ascii wide
        $rat_mutex = "AsyncMutex_" ascii wide
    condition:
        any of them
}


/*
  Rule 3: LummaC2 Infostealer — Registry Persistence and C2 Beacon Pattern
  Based on CISA Advisory AA25-141B and Microsoft Security Blog analysis of Lumma.
*/
rule Acentry_LummaC2_Infostealer {
    meta:
        author      = "CISA / Microsoft / Acentry TI"
        date        = "2026-05-20"
        description = "Detects LummaC2 infostealer via known persistence registry key pattern and reported API string artifacts"
        reference   = "https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa25-141b"
        tlp         = "CLEAR"
    strings:
        $reg_key1  = "Software\\Microsoft\\Windows\\CurrentVersion\\Run" ascii wide
        $lumma_str = "lumma" ascii nocase
        $hwid_str  = "HardwareID" ascii wide
        $steal_str = "steal_c" ascii
        $cfg_str   = "cfg_store" ascii
    condition:
        uint16(0) == 0x5A4D and
        filesize < 5MB and
        $reg_key1 and
        (
            $lumma_str or
            ($hwid_str and $steal_str) or
            $cfg_str
        )
}
```

## Recommended Actions

- [ ] **SOC — Hunt:** Search for JSP files matching XenShell patterns on all Cisco SD-WAN Controllers/Managers; review `/var/log/auth.log` for `vmanage-admin` SSH key logins from unrecognized IPs. Alert on CVE-2026-20182 exploit signatures (NETCONF manipulation post-auth-bypass).
- [ ] **SOC — Hunt:** Deploy AsyncRAT and LummaC2 YARA rules above in endpoint/EDR and network DLP tools; hunt for processes spawning `Runtime.getRuntime().exec` in Java contexts on network appliances.
- [ ] **SOC — Hunt:** Identify newly-registered domains impersonating your SSO provider (Okta, Azure AD, Duo) and VPN portals via DNS monitoring; block at web proxy before credential entry occurs.
- [ ] **SOC — Alert:** Enable detection for ClickFix-style HTML attachment behavior — obfuscated scripts executing PowerShell with `IEX` / `Invoke-Expression` on clipboard content.
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt — CRITICAL (P1):** Patch CVE-2026-20182 (Cisco SD-WAN) and CVE-2026-35616 (FortiClient EMS) immediately — both have emergency patches and confirmed active exploitation.
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt — CRITICAL (P1):** Patch CVE-2026-1281 and CVE-2026-1340 (Ivanti EPMM); if patching is delayed, isolate the EPMM appliance from the public Internet.
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt — HIGH (P2):** Apply May 2026 Exchange Server cumulative update to remediate CVE-2026-42897; consider disabling OWA if patching is delayed beyond 72h.
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt — HIGH (P2):** Apply Linux kernel patch for CVE-2026-31431 ("Copy Fail") across all server fleets; CISA federal deadline was 2026-05-15.
- [ ] **IT / MFA Hardening:** Review helpdesk identity-verification procedures; require video confirmation + manager callback for any MFA reset or account recovery. Enforce phishing-resistant MFA (FIDO2/passkeys) for privileged accounts.
- [ ] **Leadership:** Brief executive leadership on Lazarus Group "Mach-O Man" targeting of C-suite and finance roles at fintech/crypto firms; provide spear-phishing awareness refresher. Communicate elevated risk to any clients operating in telecom or critical infrastructure sectors given Salt/Volt Typhoon persistence.
