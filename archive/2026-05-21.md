Simultaneously, Chinese state-sponsored actors Salt Typhoon and Volt Typhoon continue persistent operations against U.S. telecommunications and critical infrastructure, with Salt Typhoon confirmed to have maintained access to at least one Army National Guard network for nine months. - **Active C2 Infrastructure:** ThreatFox (2026-05-12) confirmed fresh Cobalt Strike, Kimwolf botnet, ValleyRAT, and AsyncRAT C2 servers — IOCs listed below for immediate ingestion.

## APT / Threat Actor Activity

### Salt Typhoon (aliases: Earth Estries, GhostEmperor, FamousSparrow, UNC2286)

- **Targets:** U.S. and global telecommunications providers, government agencies (including military), defense contractors, critical infrastructure (energy, transportation, water), and 80+ countries.
- **TTPs:** Living-off-the-land execution via PowerShell (`T1059.001`) and WMIC (`T1047`); ACL modification to expose SSH/RDP/FTP (`T1098`); GRE and IPsec tunneling for C2 (`T1572`); credential dumping (`T1003`); lateral movement via valid accounts (`T1078`); exfiltration over C2 (`T1041`).
- **Recent activity:** DHS memo (June 2025) confirmed Salt Typhoon maintained access to a U.S. state Army National Guard network from March–December 2024, exfiltrating administrator credentials, network traffic diagrams, geographic location data, and service member PII. FBI confirmed as of February 2026 the group remains active inside U.S. telecommunications networks. Senate Commerce Committee has demanded accountability from AT&T and Verizon, suggesting telecommunications remediation is incomplete. Previously exfiltrated 1,462 network configuration files from ~70 U.S. government and critical infrastructure entities across 12 sectors.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — attributed to China's Ministry of State Security (MSS) by the FBI, CISA, and Five Eyes partners.
- **Sources:** [Dark Reading](https://www.darkreading.com/cyberattacks-data-breaches/salt-typhoon-hacks-us-national-guard), [Industrial Cyber](https://industrialcyber.co/critical-infrastructure/dhs-salt-typhoon-hackers-breached-army-national-guard-exposing-admin-credentials-and-network-diagrams/), [Senate Commerce](https://www.commerce.senate.gov/2026/2/cantwell-demands-at-t-verizon-ceos-come-clean-on-salt-typhoon-hacks-ongoing-network-security-risks)

### Volt Typhoon (aliases: VANGUARD PANDA, Bronze Silhouette, DEV-0391, Insidious Taurus, Voltzite)

- **Targets:** U.S. critical infrastructure — communications, energy, transportation, water/wastewater, defense industrial base, and U.S. territories including Guam.
- **TTPs:** Living-off-the-land binaries (LOLBins) — `wmic`, `ntdsutil`, `netsh`, PowerShell (`T1059.001`, `T1218`); valid account abuse for initial access (`T1078`); credential dumping from LSASS (`T1003.001`); encrypted C2 over legitimate protocols (`T1571`); pre-positioning on OT-adjacent IT networks; minimal malware footprint to evade detection.
- **Recent activity:** Assessed as active inside U.S. critical infrastructure OT/IT networks as of 2026, with posture consistent with pre-positioning for potential destructive or disruptive attacks rather than espionage. CybelAngel analysis (2026) confirms continued presence. MITRE ATT&CK formally tracks this group as G1017.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — attributed to the People's Republic of China (PRC) by U.S. government, Five Eyes alliance, and multiple private threat intelligence firms.
- **Sources:** [CybelAngel](https://cybelangel.com/blog/volt-typhoon/), [Eclypsium](https://eclypsium.com/blog/the-rise-of-chinese-apt-campaigns-volt-typhoon-salt-typhoon-flax-typhoon-and-velvet-ant/), [MITRE ATT&CK G1017](https://attack.mitre.org/groups/G1017/)

### Lazarus Group (aliases: HIDDEN COBRA, APT38, Zinc, Labyrinth Chollima)

- **Targets:** U.S. healthcare organizations, nonprofits (including mental health providers, educational facilities for autistic children), and Middle East targets.
- **TTPs:** Spearphishing initial access (`T1566.001`); custom malware deployment; Medusa ransomware-as-a-service adoption; credential theft; double extortion (data encryption + exfiltration); exploitation of public-facing applications (`T1190`).
- **Recent activity:** As of February 2026, Lazarus Group has adopted Medusa ransomware for extortion campaigns targeting healthcare and nonprofits — a significant tactical pivot combining North Korea's traditional cyber espionage toolkit with financially motivated ransomware operations. Nearly 30 victim organizations appeared on the Medusa data-leak site since November 2025, at least four being U.S. healthcare/nonprofits. The U.S. healthcare attempt was unsuccessful but the Middle East target was successfully encrypted.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — attributed to North Korea's Reconnaissance General Bureau (RGB) by multiple vendors; corroborated by behavioral TTPs and infrastructure overlaps.
- **Sources:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/lazarus-group-uses-medusa-ransomware-in.html), [The Register](https://www.theregister.com/2026/02/24/north_koreas_lazarus_group_healthcare_medusa_ransomware/), [Security.com](https://www.security.com/threat-intelligence/lazarus-medusa-ransomware)
