> **Daily Brief.** The 24–72 hour threat horizon remains elevated across multiple verticals. CISA added Langflow's critical RCE flaw (CVE-2025-34291, CVSS 9.4) and a Trend Micro Apex One directory-traversal exploit (CVE-2026-34926) to its KEV catalog on May 21, setting a June 4 federal remediation deadline. Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS CVE-2026-0300, a CVSS 9.3 buffer overflow in the User-ID Authentication Portal, continues to see targeted exploitation by a state-sponsored cluster (CL-STA-1132) after patches began rolling out May 13. Simultaneously, Iran-linked MuddyWater is accelerating offensive operations under "Operation Olalampo," deploying four novel malware families across MENA and adjacent regions, while China-nexus UAT-8837 maintains persistent access campaigns against North American critical infrastructure using open-source tooling and credential theft. Defenders should treat the Ivanti EPMM and Palo Alto patch guidance as highest priority and should hunt network egress for UAT-8837 Earthworm C2 infrastructure.

## Executive Summary

- **CISA KEV (May 21):** Langflow CVE-2025-34291 (CVSS 9.4, unauthenticated RCE + account takeover via CORS/CSRF chain) and Trend Micro Apex One CVE-2026-34926 (directory traversal enabling agent-push code injection) added to KEV; federal agencies must remediate by June 4, 2026.
- **Palo Alto PAN-OS CVE-2026-0300 (CVSS 9.3):** Unauthenticated root-level buffer overflow in User-ID Authentication Portal is actively exploited by CL-STA-1132, assessed as likely state-sponsored; patches rolling out through May 28 — disable external access to the Captive Portal immediately if not yet patched.
- **Ivanti EPMM triple threat:** Three Ivanti EPMM vulnerabilities now on KEV — CVE-2026-1281 / CVE-2026-1340 (CVSS 9.8, unauthenticated RCE, exploited since January 2026) and CVE-2026-6973 (CVSS 7.2, authenticated RCE chained with stolen credentials, added to KEV May 7).
- **UAT-8837 (China-nexus):** Cisco Talos documents ongoing credential harvesting and AD reconnaissance against North American critical infrastructure using Earthworm, GoTokenTheft, Certipy, and GoExec; infrastructure overlaps with SiteCore zero-day CVE-2025-53690 exploitation.
- **MuddyWater Operation Olalampo:** Group-IB documents four new malware families (CHAR Rust backdoor, GhostFetch and HTTP_VIP downloaders, GhostBackDoor) targeting MENA organizations via spear-phishing with Telegram-based C2 channels.
- **Microsoft Defender (KEV May 20):** CVE-2026-41091 (Elevation of Privilege) and CVE-2026-45498 (Denial of Service) both added to CISA KEV — apply May 2026 cumulative updates immediately.

## APT / Threat Actor Activity

### UAT-8837 (aliases: China-nexus APT, assessed PRC-affiliated)

- **Targets:** Critical infrastructure sectors in North America — energy, manufacturing, government supply chain; secondary focus on AD and PKI infrastructure
- **TTPs:** Initial access via server exploitation or stolen credentials (`T1190`, `T1078`); RDP RestrictedAdmin bypass (`T1021.001`); artifact staging in `C:\Windows\Temp\`, `C:\Users\Public\Music\`; credential theft via GoTokenTheft, Rubeus, Certipy (`T1003`, `T1558`); AD enumeration with SharpHound, dsquery, dsget (`T1069`, `T1087.002`); SOCKS5 tunneling via Earthworm to attacker C2 (`T1572`); lateral movement via GoExec/WMI (`T1047`); persistence via backdoored domain accounts (`T1136.002`)
- **Recent activity:** Multiple confirmed North American intrusions documented by Cisco Talos through January 2026; overlapping TTP and infrastructure with SiteCore CVE-2025-53690 zero-day campaign. In one intrusion, actor exfiltrated victim DLL libraries, raising supply chain trojanization risk. Operator cycles tool variants when EDR detects previous versions, indicating high operational tempo.
- **Attribution confidence:** Medium — China-nexus assessed; not yet formally named to a specific MSS/PLA unit
- **Sources:** [Cisco Talos](https://blog.talosintelligence.com/uat-8837/), [Security Affairs](https://securityaffairs.com/186999/breaking-news/china-linked-apt-uat-8837-targets-north-american-critical-infrastructure.html)

---

### MuddyWater / MERCURY (aliases: Static Kitten, Seedworm, Mango Sandstorm, TA450, Earth Vetala, TEMP.Zagros)

- **Targets:** Government, telecom, defense, and critical infrastructure across MENA region (Middle East, Turkey, Africa); secondary targeting of Israeli entities
- **TTPs:** Spear-phishing initial access (`T1566.001`); fake Quick Assist portals for credential harvesting (`T1056.003`); Microsoft Teams abuse for C2 and credential theft (`T1218`); Rust-based CHAR backdoor and RustyWater RAT (`T1059.006`); novel downloaders GhostFetch and HTTP_VIP (`T1105`); GhostBackDoor with environment-adaptive persistence (`T1543`); Telegram bot C2 channel (`T1102.002`); Chaos ransomware false-flag to mask espionage (`T1486`)
- **Recent activity:** Operation Olalampo (first observed January 26, 2026) deploys four previously undocumented malware families. Group-IB's DNS analysis of C2 infrastructure revealed four newly registered domains (Oct 2025 – Feb 2026) administered via Namecheap from Iceland. Separate RustyWater campaign targets Israeli government infrastructure. Monitoring of Telegram C2 bot provided real-time visibility into operator commands and data collection.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — Iranian MOIS-sponsored; MITRE ATT&CK Group G0069; joint CISA/FBI/CNMF attribution
- **Sources:** [Group-IB Olalampo](https://www.group-ib.com/blog/muddywater-operation-olalampo/), [Dark Reading](https://www.darkreading.com/threat-intelligence/iran-muddywater-new-malware-tensions-mount), [Rapid7](https://www.rapid7.com/blog/post/tr-muddying-tracks-state-sponsored-shadow-behind-chaos-ransomware/)

### Salt Typhoon (aliases: GhostEmperor, FamousSparrow, Earth Estries, UNC2286)

- **Targets:** Telecommunications providers globally — US carriers (AT&T, Verizon, Lumen previously confirmed), now expanding to South American telecoms; FBI and government agency adjacent networks
- **TTPs:** Long-term persistent access in carrier core networks (`T1505.003`); bulk metadata collection (`T1119`); three new 2026 implants — TernDoor, PeerTime, BruteEntry deployed against South American providers; firmware-level persistence on network devices (`T1542.005`)
- **Recent activity:** Expanded to South American telecom operators in early 2026 with three new implant families. Over 1 million individuals' metadata compromised in prior US operations. C2 infrastructure tied to ShadowPad SSL certificates (IP: 165.154.227.192). CISA published AA25-239a joint advisory on PRC telecom compromises.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — attributed to PRC MSS by Five Eyes joint advisories; CISA AA25-239a
- **Sources:** [CISA AA25-239a](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa25-239a), [Huntress](https://www.huntress.com/threat-library/threat-actors/salt-typhoon), [Censys](https://www.censys.com/blog/the-persistent-threat-of-salt-typhoon-tracking-exposures-of-potentially-targeted-devices)

## Recommended Actions

- [ ] **SOC — Hunt:** Search endpoint telemetry and firewall logs for Earthworm C2 IPs (172.188.162.183, 74.176.166.174, 20.200.129.75, 4.144.1.47, 103.235.46.102). Enable Snort SIDs 61883, 61884, 63727, 63728. Hunt for processes named `*.ico` executing from `C:\Windows\Temp\` or `C:\Users\Public\Music\` — a confirmed UAT-8837 staging TTP.
- [ ] **SOC — Block/Alert:** Add to DNS block list: `adm-pulse.com`, `moonzonet.com`, `uploadfiler.com`, `stratioai.org`, `bootcamptg.org`, `screenai.online`. Alert on outbound connections to all IPs in the IOC section above.
- [ ] **SOC — Hunt:** Review Palo Alto firewall portal-interface logs for abnormally long URL paths, repeated unauthenticated POSTs, and unusual user-agents consistent with CVE-2026-0300 scanning. Check for new root-level local accounts or unscheduled scripts on PA-Series/VM-Series appliances.
- [ ] **SOC — Detect:** Deploy the three YARA rules above to EDR/AV and file-scanning pipelines. Import all SHA-256 hashes in the IOC section into threat intel platforms and block at endpoint.
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt (P1 — Patch Immediately):** Ivanti EPMM: apply RPM 12.x.0.x/12.x.1.x for CVE-2026-1281, CVE-2026-1340, and CVE-2026-6973; rotate ALL EPMM admin credentials. Palo Alto PAN-OS: apply branch patch (rolling through May 28); restrict Captive Portal to trusted IPs now if unpatched.
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt (P1 — Federal Deadline June 4):** Langflow: upgrade to patched release (CVE-2025-34291). Trend Micro Apex One: apply May 2026 Security Bulletin patch (CVE-2026-34926).
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt (P2 — ASAP):** Apply May 2026 Windows cumulative updates to address CVE-2026-41091 (Defender EoP) and CVE-2026-45498 (Defender DoS).
- [ ] **Leadership:** Three separate Ivanti EPMM CVEs now on KEV with active chained exploitation — organizations on on-premise EPMM should evaluate migration to Ivanti Neurons for MDM (cloud, unaffected). The PAN-OS CVE-2026-0300 cluster and Microsoft Defender KEV additions indicate a broad multi-vendor patch sprint is required this week; consider emergency CAB. Advise clients in energy, telecom, and North American government sectors of elevated UAT-8837 targeting risk.
