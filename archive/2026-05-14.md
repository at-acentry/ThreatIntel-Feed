> **Daily Brief.** The dominant story 24 hours after May Patch Tuesday is a wave of fresh edge/identity disclosures landing on top of unfinished patch work: OPNsense shipped 26.1.8 last night to close an unauthenticated DHCP RCE (CVE-2026-45158, CVSS 9.1), Fortinet pushed five advisories the same evening — including a critical unauthenticated authorization bypass in FortiSandbox (CVE-2026-26083) and a separate FortiAuthenticator unauthenticated command-execution flaw (CVE-2026-44277) — and Composer 2.9.8 / 2.2.28 emergency releases address a credential-disclosure regression (GHSA-f9f8-rm49-7jv2) that was actively leaking live `GITHUB_TOKEN` values into GitHub Actions stderr until GitHub temporarily rolled back the new token format on the 13th. Meanwhile the **CVE-2026-31431 "Copy Fail" Linux KEV deadline is tomorrow (Friday, May 15)**, and the **CVE-2026-0300 PAN-OS** phased patch window opened yesterday and runs through May 28 — internet-facing PA-Series and VM-Series remain the top action item for the week. On the actor front, ANY.RUN/Quetzal Team published a full technical breakdown of DPRK Lazarus's new macOS-focused "Mach-O Man" kit (Telegram-delivered ClickFix lure, Go-based four-stage chain, Keychain and browser-credential theft), and BlueVoyant disclosed a separate, SEO-poisoning Microsoft Teams installer campaign delivering a new loader/backdoor codenamed "Lorem Ipsum" — both worth Windows and macOS hunt cycles today.

## Executive Summary

- **CVE-2026-45158 (OPNsense DHCP, CVSS 9.1) — Unauthenticated root RCE; patch released May 13.** Unsanitized user input in the DHCP configuration handler permits remote command execution as root on the firewall. Fixed in OPNsense 26.1.8; a public PoC exists. Internet-exposed OPNsense management is the highest-risk surface — patch within 24 hours.
- **CVE-2026-26083 (Fortinet FortiSandbox, FG-IR-26-136) — Critical unauthenticated missing-authorization.** Fortinet PSIRT released the advisory on May 12. FortiSandbox is an analyst/forensics product often left network-reachable inside the SOC zone; the advisory permits unauthenticated access to privileged functions. Patch immediately and audit FortiSandbox exposure.
- **CVE-2026-44277 (Fortinet FortiAuthenticator) — Unauthenticated improper access control enabling command execution.** Disclosed alongside the FortiSandbox advisory in Fortinet's May 12 batch. FortiAuthenticator is the SSO/MFA broker for many Fortinet stacks, so a compromise enables identity-layer pivot. Patches available in the May 12 release train.
- **Composer GHSA-f9f8-rm49-7jv2 — GitHub Actions tokens leaked into CI logs.** A four-year-old Composer regex collided with GitHub's new hyphenated token format; failed validation echoed the **full plaintext token** to stderr where Action's masker could not redact it. GitHub temporarily rolled back the format on May 13; Composer 2.9.8 / 2.2.28 / 1.10.28 contain the fix. Treat any token that touched a vulnerable runner since the format rollout as compromised — rotate.
- **CVE-2026-0300 (PAN-OS User-ID Auth Portal, CVSS 9.3) — Patch window day 2.** First fixed builds shipped May 13; phased rollout continues through May 28. CISA KEV. State-sponsored exploitation confirmed by Palo Alto PSIRT. Acentry's priority sequence: internet-facing PA-Series → VM-Series → internal-facing devices. Until your build lands, keep the Auth Portal restricted to trusted zones and Response Pages disabled on untrusted-zone L3 interface profiles.
- **CVE-2026-31431 "Copy Fail" (Linux kernel, CVSS 7.8) — KEV deadline TOMORROW (Friday, May 15).** All major distros have shipped fixes; container hosts and multi-tenant cloud workloads remain the highest-priority surface. Public PoC is race-free and ~732 bytes.
- **CVE-2026-20127 (Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN, CVSS 10.0) — UAT-8616 cluster still active.** Talos's UAT-8616 cluster has been exploiting this auth bypass since at least 2023, chaining it with CVE-2022-20775 via deliberate downgrade-for-LPE to obtain root. ~600 SD-WAN Manager hosts remain internet-exposed per Censys; CISA Emergency Directive 26-03 remediation deadline has long passed — anything still exposed needs verification today.
- **Lazarus "Mach-O Man" macOS kit — fresh public IOC release.** Telegram-delivered ClickFix lure → Go-Mach-O four-stage chain (`teamsSDK.bin` → `D1*.bin` profiler → `minst2.bin` persistence → `macrasv2` Keychain/browser stealer) exfiltrating to Telegram bot API. Cumulative DPRK 2026 take ≈ $577M+ across crypto sector. Hashes, IPs, and a YARA rule in the IOC section below.

## APT / Threat Actor Activity

### Lazarus Group / "Mach-O Man" (aliases: Hidden Cobra, APT38, Diamond Sleet, AppleJeus, Citrine Sleet / UNC4736 — DPRK Reconnaissance General Bureau)

- **Targets:** Fintech and crypto sector executives, developers, and decision-makers operating macOS. Quetzal Team and ANY.RUN observed direct targeting of crypto exchange and DeFi infrastructure personnel; the lure exploits compromised Telegram contacts to impersonate routine business meetings.
- **TTPs:** ClickFix social-engineering lure delivered via Telegram (`T1566.003`), user paste-and-execute of attacker-supplied shell command (`T1204.002`), ad-hoc `codesign` of fake Zoom/Teams/Meet bundles to satisfy macOS Gatekeeper (`T1553.002`), four-stage Go/Mach-O implant chain — `teamsSDK.bin` stager → `D1*.bin` profiler (`T1082`, `T1057`) → `minst2.bin` persistence via LaunchAgent at `~/Library/LaunchAgents/com.onedrive.launcher.plist` (`T1543.001`) → `macrasv2` stealer harvesting browser SQLite databases, browser extensions (Brave/Vivaldi/Opera/Chrome/Firefox/Safari), and macOS Keychain entries (`T1555.003`, `T1539`). Exfiltration is over Telegram Bot API to blend with legitimate web traffic (`T1567.002`); C2 over HTTP using the Go default User-Agent on ports 8888 / 9999.
- **Recent activity:** Quetzal Team / ANY.RUN published full IOC set and technical writeup with sandbox sessions on April 21, 2026; "Mach-O Man" remains the active distribution kit for the broader ClickFix campaign that DPRK ran in parallel with the **$285M Drift Protocol** and **$293M KelpDAO** thefts (totaling more than $577M in Q1 2026 attributed to DPRK). Operators left their Telegram bot token exposed in `macrasv2`, which has aided takedown and operator-identification efforts but does **not** mitigate already-deployed implants.
- **Attribution confidence:** **High** — TTP overlap with prior ClickFix and PyLangGhost RAT campaigns publicly attributed to Lazarus; macOS-specific tradecraft and `geniex-client` code lineage match Quetzal's earlier Lazarus tracking.
- **Sources:** [ANY.RUN — Mach-O Man writeup](https://any.run/cybersecurity-blog/lazarus-macos-malware-mach-o-man/) · [Quetzal Team / Bitso — original report](https://quetzal.bitso.com/p/north-koreas-safari-hunting-for-rats) · [CoinDesk — Lazarus Mach-O Man coverage](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2026/04/22/lazarus-group-has-become-especially-dangerous-with-new-mach-o-man-attack-certik)

### UAT-8616 (Cisco SD-WAN cluster, Talos-tracked — suspected state-sponsored, attribution undisclosed)

- **Targets:** Internet-facing Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN Manager and Controller instances. Talos characterizes victims as "high-value and critical infrastructure," with Censys observing the majority of exposed instances in the United States.
- **TTPs:** Pre-auth exploitation of **CVE-2026-20127** (peering-authentication bypass) for administrative access on SD-WAN Manager (`T1190`); deliberate **downgrade of the appliance** to a version vulnerable to CVE-2022-20775 (`T1562.001`), exploitation of the older LPE for root, then **restoration of the original firmware version** to mask the activity; tooling injection via the SD-WAN Manager's admin plane (`T1059`, `T1505`); post-compromise lateral movement into the SD-WAN data plane and downstream enterprise environments.
- **Recent activity:** Talos disclosed UAT-8616 in February 2026 with assessed exploitation back to at least 2023. CISA issued Emergency Directive 26-03 (Feb 25, 2026) requiring federal remediation within two days. As of mid-May, Censys still observes hundreds of internet-exposed SD-WAN Manager/Controller hosts — Acentry assesses opportunistic follow-on exploitation by non-state actors is now likely.
- **Attribution confidence:** **Medium-High** — Talos publicly assesses with high confidence that UAT-8616 is a sophisticated cyber threat actor; no nation-state attribution has been formally published.
- **Sources:** [Talos — UAT-8616 active exploitation](https://blog.talosintelligence.com/uat-8616-sd-wan/) · [The Hacker News — Cisco SD-WAN zero-day since 2023](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/cisco-sd-wan-zero-day-cve-2026-20127.html) · [Tenable — CVE-2026-20127 analysis](https://www.tenable.com/blog/cve-2026-20127-cisco-catalyst-sd-wan-controllermanager-zero-day-authentication-bypass)

### "Lorem Ipsum" Operator Cluster (BlueVoyant-tracked; attribution undetermined)

- **Targets:** Opportunistic — users searching for "Microsoft Teams" via search engines across at least six countries. BlueVoyant has confirmed at least one US healthcare-sector victim and assesses ongoing global SEO-poisoning operations.
- **TTPs:** SEO poisoning to surface trojanized Teams installers (`T1583.008`), MSI-driven persistence creating a `Run` key value "Microsoft Revo Health" (`T1547.001`), DLL sideloading via a binary named `Microsoft Teams Revo Helper ZnrAq.exe` loading a malicious `msvcp140.dll` (`T1574.002`), substitution-cipher decoding and XOR-encrypted shellcode stubs (`T1027.013`), JFIF-disguised C2 traffic (`T1001`), and a per-victim UUID-tracked callback architecture for victim-management.
- **Recent activity:** BlueVoyant's SOC and Threat Fusion Cell have tracked the cluster since at least February 2026; **late-April 2026** iterations introduced the DLL-sideloading variant. The campaign evolved within ten weeks from a "minimally obfuscated test build" to operationally mature loader chain — suggestive of a maturing crew rather than an established RaaS affiliate.
- **Attribution confidence:** **Low** — BlueVoyant has not publicly attributed the activity to a named actor; tradecraft is consistent with financially-motivated commodity loader operators.
- **Sources:** [BlueVoyant — Lorem Ipsum writeup](https://www.bluevoyant.com/blog/lorem-ipsum-trojanized-microsoft-teams-installers-multi-stage-loader-backdoor) · [BleepingComputer — security news index](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/)

### Salt Typhoon (aliases: GhostEmperor, FamousSparrow variants, RedMike — PRC MSS)

- **Targets:** Telecommunications providers, ISPs, internet exchange points, maritime/subsea infrastructure. Persistent compromise of US telecoms remains unresolved; FBI publicly described the threat as "still very, very much ongoing" at CyberTalks 2026 in February.
- **TTPs:** Initial access via unpatched edge appliances — Cisco IOS XE, Palo Alto NGFW, Fortinet FortiGate (`T1190`); living-off-the-land via PowerShell (`T1059.001`), WMIC (`T1047`), and registry tunneling (`T1112`); custom Go-based SFTP client for staged exfiltration (`T1048.002`); persistent modification of provider-edge routers (`T1542`); lawful-intercept infrastructure abuse for metadata harvest (`T1119`).
- **Recent activity:** No new public disclosures this week, but the Cantwell-Cruz Senate inquiry into AT&T/Verizon network security remains open; Norway PST and Singapore disclosures from Q1 2026 remain the most recent named-victim attributions. CISA AA25-239a indicators remain in-scope for hunt.
- **Attribution confidence:** **High** — joint advisory by CISA, FBI, NSA, and Five Eyes partners; PRC MSS attribution corroborated by Norway PST and Mandiant.
- **Sources:** [CISA AA25-239a](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa25-239a) · [CyberScoop — FBI: Salt Typhoon "very much ongoing"](https://cyberscoop.com/fbi-salt-typhoon-ongoing-threat-cybertalks-2026/) · [TechCrunch — global telecom tracker](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/09/salt-typhoon-china-who-has-been-hacked-global-telecom-giants/)
