> **Daily Brief.** Today's threat landscape is dominated by three converging pressures: the CISA KEV deadline (May 11) for the LiteLLM AI proxy SQL injection flaw (CVE-2026-42208) arriving as active exploitation matures, the cascading exposure of the "Copy Fail" Linux kernel privilege escalation (CVE-2026-31431) across virtually every enterprise Linux deployment, and the looming Secure Boot certificate expiration on June 26 that makes today's Microsoft Patch Tuesday arguably the most operationally critical update cycle of the year. Meanwhile, Chinese APT Salt Typhoon continues its documented global telecom espionage campaign across 80+ countries with no sign of abatement, and the ClickFix social-engineering framework is driving a renewed wave of Vidar Stealer infections via compromised WordPress infrastructure. SOC and patch management teams should treat today as a high-tempo remediation day.

## Executive Summary

- **CVE-2026-42208 (LiteLLM, CVSS 9.3) — CISA KEV deadline TODAY (May 11).** A pre-authentication SQL injection in BerriAI's LiteLLM AI proxy was weaponized within 36 hours of disclosure; federal agencies must patch to v1.83.7+ by end of day. Any organization running an internet-facing LiteLLM instance should treat this as an emergency change.
- **CVE-2026-31431 "Copy Fail" (Linux kernel, CVSS 7.8) — KEV deadline May 15.** A nine-year-old logic flaw in the kernel's cryptographic subsystem enables reliable, race-free local privilege escalation to root on virtually every Linux distribution shipped since 2017 using a 732-byte script. Cloud, container, and hybrid environments are broadly affected.
- **CVE-2026-35616 (FortiClient EMS, CVSS 9.1) — Active exploitation ongoing.** An API authentication bypass enabling unauthenticated RCE in FortiClient EMS 7.4.5–7.4.6 was actively exploited from March 31; 2,000+ exposed instances remain internet-accessible. Hotfix available.
- **Microsoft May 2026 Patch Tuesday (tomorrow, May 12) — Critical Secure Boot deadline looms.** With the original 2011 Secure Boot certificates expiring June 26 (45 days), this Patch Tuesday is the final practical deployment window before organizations face potential boot-level failures. All Windows endpoints must receive both the security updates and the renewed Secure Boot certificates.
- **Salt Typhoon (Chinese MSS) — FBI confirms ongoing global telecom espionage.** FBI publicly stated in February 2026 that Salt Typhoon threats are "still very much ongoing," with 200+ compromised organizations across 80 countries. Telecom, ISP, and critical infrastructure operators are primary targets.
- **ClickFix + Vidar Stealer campaign — Active, targeting AU and broader infrastructure.** ACSC has issued an active advisory for a ClickFix social-engineering framework distributing Vidar Stealer 2.0 via compromised WordPress sites, delivering evolved variants alongside VodkaStealer and Impure Stealer. More than 60% of samples evade standard AV at time of delivery.

## APT / Threat Actor Activity

### Salt Typhoon (aliases: RedMike, GhostEmperor, FamousSparrow variants — Chinese MSS)

- **Targets:** Global telecommunications providers, ISPs, internet exchange points, critical infrastructure; 200+ organizations across 80+ countries confirmed; specific focus on US, UK, Canada, Australia, and Indo-Pacific partners
- **TTPs:** Living-off-the-land via PowerShell (`T1059.001`), WMIC (`T1047`), registry manipulation (`T1112`), Windows service creation (`T1543.003`); exploitation of unpatched VPN appliances, firewalls, and edge routers for initial access (`T1190`); persistent modification of backbone and provider-edge routers (`T1542`); credential dumping (`T1003`); lawful intercept infrastructure access and metadata collection (`T1119`)
- **Recent activity:** FBI Director confirmed in February 2026 that Salt Typhoon remains "still very much ongoing." TechCrunch documented Salt Typhoon compromises spanning global telecom giants as recently as March 2026. Initial access consistently relies on known CVEs in VPN and firewall appliances — organizations failing to patch edge devices remain primary targets. The campaign's focus has expanded from US telco interception capability access to broader global espionage infrastructure.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — attributed to China's Ministry of State Security; joint advisory issued by CISA, FBI, NSA, and Five Eyes partners (AA25-239a)
- **Sources:** [FBI/CyberScoop](https://cyberscoop.com/fbi-salt-typhoon-ongoing-threat-cybertalks-2026/), [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/09/salt-typhoon-china-who-has-been-hacked-global-telecom-giants/), [CISA Advisory AA25-239a](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa25-239a)

### APT29 / Midnight Blizzard (aliases: Cozy Bear, UNC2452, The Dukes — Russian SVR)

- **Targets:** Western government agencies, defense contractors, technology companies, cloud service providers; strategic intelligence collection focus; currently targeting M365 environments and developer infrastructure
- **TTPs:** Spearphishing with malicious OAuth applications (`T1566.002`, `T1550.001`); password spray and credential stuffing against cloud identities (`T1110.003`); abuse of trusted OAuth flows to maintain persistent access without MFA (`T1078.004`); exfiltration of email archives and source code repositories (`T1048`); residential proxy infrastructure to blend malicious traffic with legitimate user activity (`T1090.002`)
- **Recent activity:** Midnight Blizzard leveraged credentials from the 2024 Microsoft corporate email compromise to maintain access to source code repositories and internal systems as recently as early 2025. Ongoing campaigns targeting US federal agencies and defense industrial base reported through early 2026. The group continues to refine OAuth-based persistence techniques that survive password resets.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — attributed to Russian Foreign Intelligence Service (SVR); corroborated by NSA, CISA, NCSC-UK
- **Sources:** [SC Media](https://www.scworld.com/brief/midnight-blizzard-attack-against-microsoft-impacts-us-agencies), [Infosecurity Magazine](https://www.infosecurity-magazine.com/news/russias-midnight-blizzard/), [MITRE ATT&CK APT29](https://attack.mitre.org/groups/G0016/)

- **Targets:** Broad opportunistic targeting; observed campaigns specifically against Australian infrastructure (ACSC advisory May 2026), global corporate endpoints via SEO poisoning and malvertising
- **TTPs:** ClickFix social engineering (`T1204.002`) via compromised WordPress sites presenting fake CAPTCHA or browser-update prompts; victim executes PowerShell one-liner via clipboard paste (`T1059.001`); payload delivery of Vidar Stealer 2.0 alongside VodkaStealer (C++) and Impure Stealer (.NET); stealer exfiltrates browser credentials, crypto wallets, session tokens, MFA codes (`T1539`, `T1555.003`); C2 via Telegram channels (`T1102`)

- **Sources:** [ACSC Advisory](https://www.cyber.gov.au/about-us/view-all-content/alerts-and-advisories/clickfix-distributing-vidar-stealer-via-wordpress-targeting-australian-infrastructure), [Sekoia.io IClickFix](https://blog.sekoia.io/meet-iclickfix-a-widespread-wordpress-targeting-framework-using-the-clickfix-tactic/), [ReversingLabs](https://www.reversinglabs.com/blog/clickfix-yara-rule)

## YARA Rules

```yara
/*
 * Vidar Stealer 2.0 — HTTP Exfiltration Pattern
 * Detects the multipart form-data C2 exfiltration characteristic of
 * Vidar Stealer 2.0 variants distributed via ClickFix campaigns.
 * Original analysis: Ontinue Research / Sekoia.io / ACSC
 * Acentry adaptation: Acentry Threat Intelligence Team, 2026-05-11
 */
rule Acentry_VidarStealer2_HTTP_Exfil {
    meta:
        author      = "Ontinue Research / Adapted by Acentry TI"
        date        = "2026-05-11"
        description = "Detects Vidar Stealer 2.0 HTTP C2 exfiltration via multipart form-data with characteristic field names (token, hwid, profile)"
        reference   = "https://www.ontinue.com/resource/blog-vidar-stealer-malware-analysis/"
        reference2  = "https://www.cyber.gov.au/about-us/view-all-content/alerts-and-advisories/clickfix-distributing-vidar-stealer-via-wordpress-targeting-australian-infrastructure"
        tlp         = "CLEAR"
        hash        = "see MalwareBazaar tag:vidar for current samples"
    strings:
        $mpart  = "multipart/form-data; boundary=" ascii
        $token  = "Content-Disposition: form-data; name=\"token\"" ascii
        $profile = "Content-Disposition: form-data; name=\"profile\"" ascii
        $hwid   = "Content-Disposition: form-data; name=\"hwid\"" ascii
        $file   = "Content-Disposition: form-data; name=\"file\"" ascii
        $vid_id = "1BEF0A57BE110FD467A" ascii wide
    condition:
        ($mpart and 3 of ($token, $profile, $hwid, $file)) or $vid_id
}

/*
 * LiteLLM CVE-2026-42208 — SQLi Probe Detection
 * Detects time-based blind SQL injection probes targeting LiteLLM proxy
 * Authorization header, as documented by Bishop Fox and Sysdig.
 * For use in WAF, SIEM, and proxy log analysis.
 */
rule Acentry_LiteLLM_CVE_2026_42208_SQLi_Probe {
    meta:
        author      = "Acentry Threat Intelligence Team"
        date        = "2026-05-11"
        description = "Detects SQLi probe patterns targeting CVE-2026-42208 in LiteLLM — anomalous Authorization bearer values containing SQL keywords without sk- prefix"
        reference   = "https://bishopfox.com/blog/cve-2026-42208-pre-authentication-sql-injection-in-litellm-proxy"
        reference2  = "https://www.sysdig.com/blog/cve-2026-42208-targeted-sql-injection-against-litellms-authentication-path-discovered-36-hours-following-vulnerability-disclosure"
        tlp         = "CLEAR"
    strings:
        $pgsleep    = "pg_sleep" ascii nocase
        $sqli_or    = "' OR (" ascii
        $sqli_null  = ") IS NULL --" ascii
        $litellm_fp = "litellm_credentials" ascii nocase
        $litellm_t  = "LiteLLM_VerificationToken" ascii nocase
    condition:
        ($pgsleep and $sqli_or) or ($pgsleep and $sqli_null) or ($litellm_fp or $litellm_t)
}
```
