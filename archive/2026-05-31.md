> **Daily Brief.** The final day of May 2026 closes a month defined by edge-device exploitation and developer-ecosystem compromise. The Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS zero-day (CVE-2026-0300), attributed to Chinese state-sponsored actors, continued to see active exploitation across critical infrastructure sectors in 35+ countries. The TrapDoor cross-ecosystem supply chain campaign — spanning npm, PyPI, and Crates.io — emerged as the most significant developer-targeting operation of the month, deploying credential-stealing payloads with AI-toolkit disguises. Iranian APT group Screening Serpens escalated espionage operations targeting U.S., Israeli, and UAE aerospace and defense firms. Ransomware cartel activity by Qilin and DragonForce remained elevated, while the Linux "Copy Fail" privilege-escalation flaw (CVE-2026-31431) broadened the attack surface for any threat actor with foothold access to Linux environments.

## Executive Summary

- **CVE-2026-0300 (PAN-OS RCE):** Chinese-nexus threat cluster CL-STA-1132/APT41 exploited a critical buffer overflow in PAN-OS User-ID Authentication Portal since April 9; CISA added to KEV with federal patch deadline. Over 5,400 internet-exposed VM-series firewalls remain at risk.
- **TrapDoor Supply Chain Campaign:** 34 malicious packages published to npm, PyPI, and Crates.io on May 22, targeting crypto, DeFi, and AI developers with credential-harvesting payloads that establish persistence via cron, systemd, and Git hooks.
- **Screening Serpens (UNC1549):** Iran-nexus APT deployed six new RAT variants (MiniUpdate, MiniJunk V2) against U.S., Israeli, and UAE aerospace/defense targets, fusing DLL sideloading with AppDomainManager hijacking.
- **Linux CVE-2026-31431 "Copy Fail":** Nine-year-old kernel logic bug now actively exploited; CISA KEV-listed. Enables unprivileged container escape to node-level root in Kubernetes environments via page-cache corruption.
- **Ransomware Cartel Activity:** Qilin (128 victims YTD) and DragonForce (70 victims YTD) continue to dominate the ransomware landscape; both operating under a loose cartel structure with LockBit remnants.
- **CloudZ RAT / Pheno Plugin:** Novel Windows malware toolkit active since January 2026 intercepting SMS OTPs via Microsoft Phone Link, effectively bypassing MFA without targeting mobile devices directly.

## APT / Threat Actor Activity

### Screening Serpens (aliases: UNC1549, Smoke Sandstorm, Iranian Dream Job)

- **Targets:** Aerospace, defense manufacturing, and telecommunications organizations in the U.S., Israel, UAE, and two additional Middle Eastern entities.
- **TTPs:** Highly tailored spearphishing using fake job requisitions and spoofed video-conferencing invitations (`T1566.001`); DLL sideloading fused with AppDomainManager hijacking (`T1574.002`); deployment of six new RAT variants (MiniUpdate, MiniJunk V2) enabling persistent remote access (`T1059.003`).
- **Recent activity:** Campaigns observed February–April 2026, intensifying post-February 28 regional conflict. Six RAT samples attributed to two distinct families; earliest samples cluster around aerospace sector targets using job-seeker lures tailored to defense manufacturing roles.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — Unit 42 attribution to Iranian MOIS-affiliated cluster, consistent with prior UNC1549 TTPs and infrastructure.
- **Sources:** [Unit 42](https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/tracking-iran-apt-screening-serpens/), [Jerusalem Post](https://www.jpost.com/middle-east/iran-news/article-897154)

### APT41 / CL-STA-1132 (aliases: Winnti, Barium, Double Dragon)

- **Targets:** Broad critical infrastructure — automotive, defense industrial base, energy, financial services, government, healthcare, telco, transportation across 35+ countries (North America, Europe, Asia-Pacific heavily represented).
- **TTPs:** Exploitation of internet-facing edge devices (CVE-2026-0300 PAN-OS); log deletion and forensic cleanup post-exploitation (`T1070.001`); deployment of Earthworm and ReverseSocks5 tunneling tools for covert C2 (`T1572`); living-off-the-land lateral movement (`T1021`).
- **Recent activity:** Exploitation of CVE-2026-0300 began April 9, 2026; successful RCE achieved April 16; post-exploitation activity includes staged persistence and network tunneling. Campaign bears hallmarks of Chinese state-sponsored activity; CISA mandated federal patching by May 9.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — infrastructure overlaps with APT41/Volt Typhoon; Earthworm tooling consistent with known Chinese threat clusters.
- **Sources:** [F5 Labs](https://www.f5.com/labs/articles/weekly-threat-bulletin-may-13th-2026), [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/palo-alto-zero-day-exploited-in-campaign-bearing-hallmarks-of-chinese-state-hacking/), [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/pan-os-firewall-rce-zero-day-exploited-in-attacks-since-april-9/)

### Salt Typhoon (aliases: FamousSparrow, GhostEmperor)

- **Targets:** Telecommunications companies globally; over 200 organizations across 80 countries compromised since 2023; expanded to Norway and Singapore (February 2026 disclosures).
- **TTPs:** Exploitation of known CVEs for initial access to telco edge infrastructure (`T1190`); credential harvesting and persistent access to lawful intercept systems; long-dwell espionage with minimal forensic footprint (`T1036`).
- **Recent activity:** FBI confirmed ongoing active threats in February 2026. Basic configuration errors and known CVEs continue to provide initial entry points. Operational scope described as the most geographically distributed APT operation ever publicly disclosed.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — FBI, CISA, and multiple Five Eyes partners have attributed Salt Typhoon to Chinese state-sponsored actors.
- **Sources:** [CISA China Threat Advisory](https://www.cisa.gov/topics/cyber-threats-and-advisories/nation-state-cyber-actors/china), [Tenable](https://www.tenable.com/blog/salt-typhoon-an-analysis-of-vulnerabilities-exploited-by-this-state-sponsored-actor)

### Qilin / DragonForce Ransomware Cartel

- **Targets:** Healthcare, manufacturing, construction, hospitality, legal services; mid-to-large enterprises globally.
- **TTPs:** RaaS affiliate model; double extortion (encryption + data theft); Qilin leverages VMware ESXi encryptors (`T1486`); DragonForce 80% revenue-share affiliate program; cartel-style coordination with LockBit remnants.
- **Recent activity:** Qilin leads YTD with 128 victims; DragonForce at 70 victims; both groups identified as primary drivers of 31 new victims in the most recent tracking window. DragonForce gained notoriety for attacks on UK retailers (M&S, Co-op, Harrods) in 2025.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — ransomware.live tracking; Check Point Research Q1 2026 state-of-ransomware report.
- **Sources:** [Dark Reading](https://www.darkreading.com/cyberattacks-data-breaches/extortion-gangs-join-forces-ransomware-cartel), [Check Point Research](https://research.checkpoint.com/2026/the-state-of-ransomware-q1-2026/)

### TrapDoor — Cross-Ecosystem Credential-Stealing Campaign

- **Attack type:** Malicious package publication across multiple ecosystems; postinstall hook abuse; import-time payload execution; remote JavaScript payload delivery via attacker-controlled GitHub Pages.
- **Ecosystem:** npm, PyPI, Crates.io
- **Package(s) affected:** 34 packages across 384 versions. npm: `async-pipeline-builder`, `build-scripts-utils`, `chain-key-validator`, `crypto-credential-scanner`, `defi-env-auditor`, `defi-threat-scanner`, `eth-wallet-sentinel`, `llm-context-compressor`, `mnemonic-safety-check`, `model-switch-router`, `node-setup-helpers`, `project-init-tools`, `prompt-engineering-toolkit`, `solidity-deploy-guard`, `token-usage-tracker`, `wallet-backup-verifier`, `wallet-security-checker`, `web3-secrets-detector`, `workspace-config-loader`, `deployment-key-auditor`, `dev-env-bootstrapper`, `solidity-deploy-guard`. PyPI: `cryptowallet-safety`, `data-pipeline-check`, `defi-risk-scanner`, `env-loader-cli`, `eth-security-auditor`, `git-config-sync`, `solidity-build-guard`. Crates.io: `move-analyzer-build`, `move-compiler-tools`, `move-project-builder`, `sui-framework-helpers`, `sui-move-build-helper`, `sui-sdk-build-utils`.
- **Impact:** Credential harvesting of GitHub/npm tokens, CI/CD secrets, cloud credentials (AWS validation confirmed), SSH keys, browser data, crypto wallets; SSH-based lateral movement; persistence via cron, systemd, Git hooks, shell hooks, and `.cursorrules`/`CLAUDE.md` injection targeting AI coding assistants. Rust crates encrypt and exfiltrate local keystores to GitHub Gists.
- **IOCs:** C2 payload host: `ddjidd564.github.io`. Shared npm payload: `trap-core.js`.
- **Mitigation:** Remove any of the listed packages immediately (`npm uninstall <pkg>` / `pip uninstall <pkg>` / `cargo remove <crate>`); audit `.cursorrules`, `CLAUDE.md`, and Git hooks in all repositories for unauthorized content; rotate all secrets accessible from affected environments (GitHub tokens, AWS keys, SSH keys); regenerate lock files; review CI/CD pipeline logs from May 22, 2026 onwards.
- **Source:** [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/05/trapdoor-supply-chain-attack-spreads.html), [Socket.dev](https://socket.dev/blog/trapdoor-crypto-stealer-npm-pypi-crates)

### Mini Shai-Hulud — TanStack / Mistral AI GitHub Actions CI Compromise

- **Attack type:** CI/CD pipeline injection via compromised GitHub Actions workflow; self-propagating npm worm.
- **Ecosystem:** npm, PyPI; GitHub Actions.
- **Package(s) affected:** TanStack (all `@tanstack/*` packages — 42 packages, 84 malicious artifacts published within six minutes of initial compromise), Mistral AI, UiPath, and 160+ additional packages.
- **Impact:** Credential theft (developer secrets); self-propagation through the npm ecosystem; persistent destructive daemon capable of wiping developer home directories. Attributed to threat actor group TeamPCP.
- **IOCs:** No confirmed public C2 infrastructure published at time of writing.
- **Mitigation:** Audit all `@tanstack/*` dependencies and pin to versions published before May 12, 2026; verify integrity of `@mistralai/*` and `uipath` package versions; enforce GitHub Actions pinning by commit SHA rather than mutable tag references; implement Supply-chain Levels for Software Artifacts (SLSA) controls for CI workflows.
- **Source:** [Orca Security](https://orca.security/resources/blog/tanstack-npm-supply-chain-worm/), [OX Security](https://www.ox.security/blog/shai-hulud-here-we-go-again-170-packages-hit-across-npm-pypi/)
