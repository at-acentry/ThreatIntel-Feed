> **Daily Brief.** The Check Point Remote Access VPN zero-day (CVE-2026-50751), now confirmed linked to Qilin ransomware affiliates, continues to be the dominant operational threat — with active exploitation observed since early May and dozens of organizations impacted globally. SAP's June patch cycle addressed four critical-severity flaws including a CVSS 9.9 XML Signature Wrapping vulnerability and a CVSS 9.8 unauthenticated memory corruption bug in the ABAP kernel. On the AI infrastructure front, CISA added the LiteLLM command-injection flaw (CVE-2026-42271) to the KEV catalog; when chained with a Starlette host-header bypass, the attack becomes a CVSS 10.0 unauthenticated RCE. The Miasma supply chain worm — a new variant of Shai-Hulud — compromised 32 Red Hat npm packages on June 1, targeting CI/CD pipelines and cloud credentials across tens of thousands of weekly installs.

## Executive Summary

- **Check Point VPN zero-day (CVE-2026-50751, CVSS 9.3)** confirmed exploited by Qilin ransomware affiliate since May 7; hotfix available but four affected version branches are already end-of-support; attacker infrastructure tied to Kaupo Cloud HK, Shock Hosting, and Vultr.
- **LiteLLM KEV addition (CVE-2026-42271)**: command injection in MCP server endpoints chains with Starlette host-header bypass (CVE-2026-48710) for unauthenticated RCE against AI gateway deployments; update to LiteLLM 1.83.7+ and Starlette 1.0.1+.
- **SAP June patch day**: four critical flaws patched including CVE-2026-44748 (CVSS 9.9, ABAP XML Signature Wrapping) and CVE-2026-27671 (CVSS 9.8, unauthenticated memory corruption via RFC) — emergency patching warranted for internet-exposed SAP environments.
- **Cisco SD-WAN Manager zero-day (CVE-2026-20245, CVSS 7.8)**: unpatched root privilege escalation under active exploitation; this is Cisco's seventh SD-WAN zero-day in 2026 — no patch available; network-level isolation is the only current mitigation.
- **Miasma/Shai-Hulud supply chain worm** compromised 32 @redhat-cloud-services npm packages (96 malicious versions, 116k+ weekly downloads); a simultaneous PyPI variant ("Hades") hit ~24 science-focused packages; both exfiltrate cloud and CI/CD credentials via GitHub repository drops.
- **FIFA World Cup 2026 phishing surge**: 1,100+ spoofed domains identified by Insikt Group; FBI IC3 has published an official domain blocklist (PSA260527) ahead of the June 11 kickoff.

## APT / Threat Actor Activity

### Screening Serpens (aliases: UNC1549, Smoke Sandstorm, Iranian Dream Job)

- **Targets:** Technology, aerospace, defense manufacturing, and telecommunications sectors in the United States, Israel, the United Arab Emirates, and other Middle Eastern entities.
- **TTPs:** Spear-phishing lures mimicking job recruitment portals for initial access (`T1566.001`); DLL sideloading for execution (`T1574.002`); AppDomainManager hijacking to disable ETW and strong-name validation prior to application launch (`T1574.014`); unique per-target C2 clusters (3–5 Azure-hosted domains each) to limit cross-contamination (`T1583.006`); deployment of MiniUpdate and MiniJunk V2 RAT families (`T1059.003`).
- **Recent activity:** Between mid-February and April 2026, Screening Serpens deployed six new RAT variants across coordinated campaigns, sharply increasing operational tempo following the regional conflict beginning February 28, 2026. Unit 42 observed at least five affected countries.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — PRC-nexus infrastructure overlap with known Iranian state-sponsored actors; campaign timing aligns with regional geopolitical events.
- **Sources:** [Unit 42](https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/tracking-iran-apt-screening-serpens/), [Cyber Magazine](https://cybermagazine.com/news/unit-42-shows-how-iranian-hackers-hide-behind-job-postings)

### Phantom Taurus (aliases: None publicly confirmed)

- **Targets:** Ministries of foreign affairs, embassies, military operations, government agencies, and telecommunications providers across Africa, the Middle East, and Asia.
- **TTPs:** Custom fileless NET-STAR backdoor enabling covert long-term access (`T1027`); SQL database targeting via mssq.bat credential-based queries with CSV exfiltration (`T1005`); shared Chinese APT operational infrastructure overlapping with Iron Taurus (APT27), Starchy Taurus (Winnti), and Stately Taurus (Mustang Panda); email → database pivot observed since early 2025 (`T1560.001`).
- **Recent activity:** Phantom Taurus has maintained persistent access across government and telecom environments spanning the past two and a half years. The group is characterized by surgical precision and the use of techniques not previously observed in other Chinese APT operations. Dark Reading reported on the group's continued expansion in June 2026.
- **Attribution confidence:** High — infrastructure exclusively used by Chinese threat actors; custom tooling aligns with PRC strategic intelligence collection priorities.
- **Sources:** [Unit 42](https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/phantom-taurus/), [Dark Reading](https://www.darkreading.com/cyberattacks-data-breaches/new-china-apt-strikes-precision-persistence), [Security Affairs](https://securityaffairs.com/182852/apt/china-linked-apt-phantom-taurus-uses-net-star-malware-in-espionage-campaigns-against-key-sectors.html)

### Qilin (aliases: Agenda Ransomware)

- **Targets:** Critical infrastructure, education, satellite communications, and business services sectors; confirmed victims in Australia (Kinetic Education) and US (SatCom CX) in early June 2026.
- **TTPs:** Exploitation of internet-facing VPN appliances for initial access (`T1190`); use of Rclone for data staging and exfiltration prior to encryption (`T1560`); Tox protocol for C2 communications (`T1071.001`); VPS infrastructure rotated across Kaupo Cloud HK, Shock Hosting, and Vultr Holdings (`T1583.003`).
- **Recent activity:** A Qilin affiliate exploited CVE-2026-50751 (Check Point VPN zero-day) beginning May 7, 2026, with campaign acceleration in early June. Check Point confirmed limited scope — several dozen organizations — but Rapid7 MDR has independently observed at least one high-confidence confirmed intrusion. Two ransomware victims claimed on June 8, 2026.
- **Attribution confidence:** Medium — Check Point and Rapid7 assess Qilin affiliation based on binary analysis and post-exploitation TTPs; full confirmation pending forensic analysis.
- **Sources:** [Help Net Security](https://www.helpnetsecurity.com/2026/06/08/check-point-cve-2026-50751-qilin-ransomware/), [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/check-point-vpn-zero-day-exploited-in-qilin-ransomware-attacks/), [DeXpose](https://www.dexpose.io/qilin-ransomware/)

### @redhat-cloud-services npm packages — Miasma (Shai-Hulud Variant)

- **Attack type:** Compromised maintainer account / malicious update
- **Ecosystem:** npm
- **Package(s) affected:** 32 packages under the `@redhat-cloud-services` npm scope; 96 total malicious versions; approximately 116,991 cumulative weekly downloads
- **Impact:** The Miasma payload (a 4.2 MB obfuscated script executed via npm `preinstall` hook) harvests GitHub personal access tokens, npm publishing tokens, AWS credentials, Kubernetes secrets, SSH keys, HashiCorp Vault tokens, and other CI/CD secrets present on the installing host or runner. Stolen credentials are uploaded encrypted to attacker-controlled GitHub repositories. The worm self-propagates by using stolen npm tokens to publish additional infected versions.
- **IOCs:** Malicious package names include versions across all 32 `@redhat-cloud-services` scope packages published on or after June 1, 2026. Approximately 400 credential-dump GitHub repositories have been created by the worm as of reporting. Specific malicious package version strings: see OX Security advisory.
- **Mitigation:** Audit all `@redhat-cloud-services` package versions installed on or after June 1, 2026. Rotate all GitHub PATs, npm tokens, AWS IAM keys, and Kubernetes secrets accessible from any CI/CD environment that ran an `npm install` during the exposure window. Verify SLSA provenance attestations cannot be sole trust anchor — Miasma was the first campaign confirmed to compromise packages carrying valid SLSA Build Level 3 attestations. Pin to verified-clean pre-June-1 versions while Red Hat completes remediation.
- **Source:** [Aikido Security](https://www.aikido.dev/blog/red-hat-npm-packages-compromised-credential-stealing-worm), [OX Security](https://www.ox.security/blog/shai-hulud-here-we-go-again-170-packages-hit-across-npm-pypi/), [SecurityWeek](https://www.securityweek.com/over-100-npm-pypi-packages-hit-in-new-shai-hulud-supply-chain-attacks/)

### PyPI Science Packages — Hades (Shai-Hulud Variant)

- **Attack type:** Compromised maintainer account / malicious update
- **Ecosystem:** PyPI
- **Package(s) affected:** Approximately 24 science-focused PyPI packages; collectively hundreds of thousands of weekly downloads. The variant is identified internally by the string "Hades – The End for the Damned."
- **Impact:** Identical credential-theft behavior to Miasma — exfiltrates developer secrets and CI/CD credentials on install or import. Encrypted stolen credentials uploaded to attacker GitHub repositories.
- **IOCs:** Package names not yet publicly disclosed in full; security researchers at Checkmarx and BleepingComputer are tracking the full list. Approximately 359 attacker-controlled GitHub repositories created for credential drops associated with this variant.
- **Mitigation:** Audit recently-updated PyPI dependencies in data science, ML, and scientific computing projects. Run `pip-audit` across affected environments. Rotate all secrets accessible from any Python environment that imported new package versions since late May 2026.
- **Source:** [BleepingComputer](https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-shai-hulud-attack-trojanizes-19-science-focused-pypi-packages/), [NHS England Digital](https://digital.nhs.uk/cyber-alerts/2026/cc-4781)

## Recommended Actions

- [ ] **SOC:** Hunt for CVE-2026-50751 exploitation activity in Check Point VPN logs dating back to May 7, 2026 — review IKEv1 authentication sessions, look for connections from the 11 published Qilin infrastructure IPs, and correlate with any unexpected Rclone or Tox protocol traffic. Enable IOC-based detections for the two published file hashes.
- [ ] **SOC:** Sweep for CVE-2026-42271 exploitation attempts against any internally-exposed LiteLLM instances — look for anomalous POST requests to `/mcp-rest/test/connection` and `/mcp-rest/test/tools/list` endpoints, especially with `stdio` transport parameters. Correlate with unusual subprocess spawning.
- [ ] **SOC:** Block the 11 Qilin-attributed IPs and 34 FIFA phishing domains at perimeter/DNS immediately. Ingest FBI IC3 PSA260527 domain list into SIEM and SOAR blocklists.
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt:** (1) Apply Check Point hotfix for CVE-2026-50751 on an emergency basis across all Remote Access VPN, Mobile Access, and Spark Firewall deployments — prioritize migration away from end-of-support R80.x and R81 branches. (2) Deploy SAP June 2026 Security Patches addressing CVE-2026-44748, CVE-2026-27671, CVE-2026-40128, and CVE-2026-22732 — treat internet-exposed SAP NetWeaver as P1. (3) Upgrade LiteLLM to 1.83.7+ and Starlette to 1.0.1+. (4) For Cisco SD-WAN Manager CVE-2026-20245 — no patch available; isolate management interfaces at the network layer and restrict access to trusted admin IPs only. (5) Apply or verify PAN-OS GlobalProtect mitigations for CVE-2026-0257 (disable auth override cookies or enforce certificate separation).
- [ ] **Dev / AppSec:** (1) Immediately audit all `@redhat-cloud-services` npm dependencies — any version installed on or after June 1, 2026 should be considered compromised until verified clean. (2) Rotate all CI/CD secrets, GitHub PATs, npm tokens, AWS IAM keys, and Kubernetes secrets accessible from any runner that may have executed an `npm install` since June 1. (3) Audit recently-updated science/data PyPI packages for Hades variant indicators. (4) Do not rely solely on SLSA Build Level 3 provenance as a trust signal — Miasma has demonstrated it can be defeated. Review supply chain policy for compensating controls.
- [ ] **Leadership:** The Check Point VPN zero-day linked to Qilin ransomware represents the most acute client-facing risk this cycle. Clients running Check Point Remote Access VPN, especially on end-of-support firmware, should receive a proactive advisory if not already patched. The LiteLLM vulnerability affecting AI gateway infrastructure may warrant disclosure to clients in financial services and healthcare with AI-augmented SOC or chat tooling deployments.
