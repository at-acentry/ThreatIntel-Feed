> **Daily Brief.** Today's threat landscape is dominated by Palo Alto Networks' disclosure of a critical, actively exploited unauthenticated RCE in PAN-OS User-ID Authentication Portal (CVE-2026-0300, CVSS 9.3) with no fixed builds available until tomorrow — Acentry assesses this as the highest-priority operational exposure of the week. In parallel, CISA's KEV deadline for the Windows Shell zero-click NTLM coercion flaw (CVE-2026-32202) hits today and the "Copy Fail" Linux kernel LPE deadline arrives Friday, while Microsoft's May Patch Tuesday rolls out tonight with the Secure Boot certificate renewal client teams have been preparing for since April. Russia-linked APT28 continues weaponising CVE-2026-32202 in Operation Neusploit follow-on activity against Eastern European government and logistics targets, and Cisco SD-WAN customers should treat UAT-8616 post-exploitation cleanup (CVE-2026-20127) as a hunt-driven, not patch-driven, problem given evidence of root persistence dating to 2023.

## Executive Summary

- **CVE-2026-0300 (PAN-OS User-ID Auth Portal, CVSS 9.3) — Active exploitation; no patch until May 13–28.** Palo Alto Networks confirmed limited, likely state-sponsored exploitation of an unauthenticated buffer overflow yielding root RCE on PA-Series and VM-Series firewalls when the Authentication Portal (Captive Portal) is exposed to untrusted networks. Mitigations (restrict portal access to trusted zones; disable Response Pages) are the only available remediation today.
- **CVE-2026-32202 (Windows Shell, CVSS 4.3) — CISA KEV deadline TODAY (May 12).** A zero-click LNK-driven NTLM coercion flaw — an incomplete patch of an earlier APT28 zero-day — is being actively exploited to harvest Net-NTLMv2 hashes for relay and lateral movement. April Patch Tuesday update closes it.
- **CVE-2026-31431 "Copy Fail" (Linux kernel, CVSS 7.8) — KEV deadline May 15 (Friday).** Logic flaw in `algif_aead` allows a 732-byte unprivileged script to escalate to root on virtually every Linux distribution since 2017. Patches are available across all major distros; container and multi-tenant environments are highest priority.
- **Microsoft May 2026 Patch Tuesday (today) — Secure Boot cert rollout final window.** Original 2011 Secure Boot certificates expire June 26 (45 days); today's cumulative update is the last practical chance to deploy the renewed certs at scale before boot-level fallout becomes likely.
- **CVE-2026-23918 (Apache HTTP/2 mod_http2, CVSS 8.8) — PoC public; DoS trivial, RCE feasible.** Double-free in stream cleanup on Apache 2.4.66; weaponized DoS works against any default mod_http2 deployment with no auth. Upgrade to 2.4.67 as an emergency change.
- **APT28 (Forest Blizzard / Fancy Bear) — Operation Neusploit follow-on activity continues.** Russian SVR-linked targeting of Romania, Slovakia, Ukraine government and logistics bodies via RTF-based CVE-2026-21509 chain (MiniDoor, PixyNetLoader, Covenant Grunt) plus CVE-2026-32202 NTLM coercion. Operation Neusploit IOCs published by Zscaler ThreatLabz remain operationally relevant.

## APT / Threat Actor Activity

### APT28 (aliases: Fancy Bear, Forest Blizzard, Pawn Storm, GruesomeLarch, Sednit, STRONTIUM — Russian GRU Unit 26165)

- **Targets:** Government, defense, logistics, and transport sectors in Romania, Slovakia, Ukraine; broader Central and Eastern European public sector; expanding into Western European logistics providers supporting Ukraine military aid pipelines.
- **TTPs:** Spearphishing with weaponized RTF attachments (`T1566.001`) exploiting CVE-2026-21509; multi-stage in-memory dropper DLL execution (`T1055`); deployment of **MiniDoor** Outlook VBA project for email exfiltration via auto-forwarding (`T1114.001`); **PixyNetLoader** in-memory loader for **Covenant Grunt** C2 beacon (`T1071.001`); zero-click NTLM coercion via CVE-2026-32202 LNK shortcuts on shared file servers (`T1187`); NTLM relay (`T1557.001`) and offline cracking of harvested Net-NTLMv2 hashes (`T1110.002`).
- **Recent activity:** Operation Neusploit IOCs (Zscaler, Jan 2026) remain operationally relevant; follow-on Microsoft and Akamai telemetry confirms continued use of CVE-2026-32202 through April 2026. APT28 weaponized CVE-2026-32202 within 14 days of the (incomplete) February patch, signaling sustained zero-day pipeline against Office/Shell parsing surfaces.
- **Attribution confidence:** **High** — multi-vendor consensus (Microsoft, CrowdStrike, Mandiant, Zscaler, Akamai, Sophos); long-standing GRU 26165 attribution; Operation Neusploit infrastructure overlap with prior Fighting Ursa campaigns.
- **Sources:** [Zscaler ThreatLabz — Operation Neusploit](https://www.zscaler.com/blogs/security-research/apt28-leverages-cve-2026-21509-operation-neusploit) · [Akamai — CVE-2026-32202 deep dive](https://www.akamai.com/blog/security-research/incomplete-patch-apt28s-zero-day-cve-2026-32202) · [MITRE ATT&CK G0007](https://attack.mitre.org/groups/G0007/)

### Salt Typhoon (aliases: GhostEmperor, FamousSparrow variants, RedMike — Chinese MSS)

- **Targets:** Telecommunications providers, ISPs, internet exchange points, and maritime/subsea infrastructure operators; campaigns documented across 80+ countries. Norway publicly attributed Salt Typhoon to compromises of national telecom and maritime targets in February 2026.
- **TTPs:** Initial access via unpatched edge appliances — Cisco IOS XE, Palo Alto NGFW, Fortinet FortiGate (`T1190`); living-off-the-land via PowerShell (`T1059.001`), WMIC (`T1047`), registry tunneling (`T1112`); custom Go-based SFTP client for staged exfiltration (`T1048.002`); persistent modification of provider-edge routers (`T1542`); lawful-intercept infrastructure abuse for metadata harvest (`T1119`).
- **Recent activity:** Norwegian PST publicly confirmed Salt Typhoon compromise of Norwegian critical infrastructure in February 2026, the first European public attribution. Darktrace observed C2 to `thetavaluemetrics[.]com` (74.91.125[.]57) in a late-2025 intrusion that aligns with Salt Typhoon TTPs. CISA AA25-239a indicators remain in-scope for hunt across telecom and maritime defense networks.
- **Attribution confidence:** **High** — joint advisory by CISA, FBI, NSA, Five Eyes; PRC MSS attribution corroborated by Norway PST, GCA, and Silent Push infrastructure mapping.
- **Sources:** [CISA AA25-239a](https://www.cisa.gov/news-events/cybersecurity-advisories/aa25-239a) · [The Record — Norway Disclosure](https://therecord.media/norawy-intelligence-discloses-salt-typhoon-attacks) · [Darktrace — Salt Typhoon Intrusion](https://www.darktrace.com/blog/salty-much-darktraces-view-on-a-recent-salt-typhoon-intrusion)

### UAT-8616 (Cisco SD-WAN Operator; provisional designation — Cisco Talos)

- **Targets:** Telecommunications, energy, manufacturing, and government Cisco Catalyst SD-WAN (vManage/vSmart) deployments. Long-dwell (≥3 years) presence concentrated in US, EMEA, and APAC critical-infrastructure verticals.
- **TTPs:** Exploitation of CVE-2026-20127 for unauthenticated admin access (`T1190`); downgrade-attack to CVE-2022-20775 for root escalation (`T1068`); revert-and-restore software version to bury forensic trail (`T1070`); SSH `authorized_keys` implant for persistence (`T1098.004`); modification of SD-WAN startup scripts (`T1037`); systematic log purge across `/var/log`, shell history, and connection tables (`T1070.002`, `T1070.003`).
- **Recent activity:** Cisco Talos disclosed UAT-8616 alongside the February 2026 CVE-2026-20127 advisory; intrusions confirmed back to 2023. CISA mandated FCEB remediation within 24 hours of KEV listing — an unusually aggressive deadline reflecting the long-dwell, root-level nature of the access.
- **Attribution confidence:** **Medium-High** — Cisco Talos assesses UAT-8616 as a "highly sophisticated cyber threat actor"; specific nation-state attribution not yet public. Tradecraft and target set are consistent with PRC strategic access operations but unconfirmed.
- **Sources:** [Cisco Talos — UAT-8616](https://blog.talosintelligence.com/uat-8616-sd-wan/) · [The Hacker News](https://thehackernews.com/2026/02/cisco-sd-wan-zero-day-cve-2026-20127.html) · [Cisco SD-WAN Advisory](https://sec.cloudapps.cisco.com/security/center/content/CiscoSecurityAdvisory/cisco-sa-sdwan-authbp-qwCX8D4v)

### Lazarus Group / DPRK Reconnaissance General Bureau (aliases: Hidden Cobra, APT38, Diamond Sleet, Andariel overlap)

- **Targets:** Nuclear-sector engineers and supply-chain personnel; defense, aerospace, and cryptocurrency targets continuing in parallel.
- **TTPs:** Operation DreamJob social engineering (`T1566.003`) — fake job-assessment archives delivered via LinkedIn/email; **Ranid Downloader** initial-stage payload retrieves Base64-encoded, RSA-encrypted second stage; **CookiePlus** modular plugin-based memory-resident backdoor masquerading as the `ComparePlus` Notepad++ plugin (`T1574.001`); shellcode/DLL execution from C2-decoded payload; lives entirely in memory once injected (`T1620`).

The shift from financial to nuclear/defense intelligence targets aligns with broader DPRK strategic priorities.
- **Attribution confidence:** **High** — DPRK Reconnaissance General Bureau attribution sustained across Kaspersky, Mandiant, Microsoft, and US Treasury sanctions.
- **Sources:** [Kaspersky — CookiePlus](https://www.kaspersky.com/about/press-releases/kaspersky-discovers-lazarus-apt-targets-nuclear-organizations-with-new-cookieplus-malware) · [Orange Cyberdefense — DreamJob Arsenal](https://www.orangecyberdefense.com/fileadmin/global/Blog/Navigating_Operation_DreamJob_s_arsenal_5.pdf) · [MITRE ATT&CK G0032](https://attack.mitre.org/groups/G0032/)

## Recommended Actions

- [ ] **SOC — PAN-OS (CVE-2026-0300):** Inventory all PA-Series / VM-Series firewalls with Authentication Portal enabled; alert on any HTTP traffic from untrusted zones to the portal listener; pull packet captures of portal traffic and retain for forensic review; enable enhanced logging on `interfaceMgmt` profiles facing untrusted zones.
- [ ] **SOC — Windows Shell (CVE-2026-32202):** Hunt for outbound SMB to non-corporate IPs (especially from user workstations); search EDR for `Explorer.exe` spawning network connections to TCP 445 outside the corporate range; block egress SMB at the perimeter; review honey-LNK telemetry on share root paths.
- [ ] **SOC — Cisco SD-WAN (CVE-2026-20127 / UAT-8616):** Treat as compromise-assumed. Audit SSH `authorized_keys`, startup scripts, and `/var/log` integrity on every Internet-exposed vManage; reimage suspect hosts after evidence preservation; do **not** rely solely on patching.
- [ ] **SOC — APT28 (Operation Neusploit):** Deploy the MiniDoor YARA rule against Outlook mailbox PSTs and OST caches; alert on the two `ahmeclaw*` exfil mailboxes; review mail-flow rules for unauthorized auto-forwards.
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt — Linux (Copy Fail, CVE-2026-31431):** Deploy distro kernel updates today, prioritising multi-tenant hosts, Kubernetes nodes, and any system with untrusted local users. Interim: blacklist `algif_aead` via `/etc/modprobe.d/`.
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt — Microsoft May 2026 Patch Tuesday:** Schedule cumulative deployment across the Windows fleet tonight; confirm Secure Boot certificate refresh component is present in the build catalog; communicate the June 26 cert-expiration risk to leadership.
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt — Apache (CVE-2026-23918):** Identify all Apache 2.4.x web servers with `mod_http2` enabled; emergency-change to 2.4.67; if patching blocked, disable HTTP/2 temporarily.
- [ ] **IT / Patch Mgmt — Office (CVE-2026-21509):** Verify January 2026 cumulative Office updates deployed everywhere; enforce Protected View for all RTF/Office docs from email and internet zones.
- [ ] **Leadership / Client Comms:** Brief executive stakeholders on the PAN-OS exposure (state-sponsored, no patch yet) and the operational implications for any client running Authentication Portal externally; surface the Secure Boot June 26 deadline as a board-level scheduling risk.
